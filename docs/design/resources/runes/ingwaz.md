## Pre-Glitch Origins: The Perfect Storage

### Original Function

In the Age of Forging, Ingwaz (ᛜ) served as the root function for potential-state preservation—the storage of energy, data, genetic templates, and resources in a compressed "seed-state" that could be released when conditions were met. Unlike Fehu (ᚠ), which governed active resource flow, Ingwaz governed **dormant potential**—the space between accumulation and deployment.

| Operational Theater | Function |
|---------------------|----------|
| **Seed-Vaults** | Genetic and data template preservation; "perfect copies" in stasis |
| **Energy Capacitors** | Aetheric potential storage; controlled discharge on demand |
| **Gestation Chambers** | Biological development environments; growth-stage management |
| **Cycle Completion** | Process-state preservation; "save points" for complex operations |
| **Resource Reserves** | Strategic stockpiling; release-on-trigger protocols |

### The Dormant Power

Ingwaz's distinctive characteristic was its operation through **containment rather than action**:

| System | Function |
|--------|----------|
| **Potential Compression** | Command: "Hold this energy/data/resource in ready-state" |
| **Gestation Management** | Controlled internal development without external expression |
| **Release-Gate Integration** | Conditional triggers for discharge (time, signal, threshold) |
| **Cycle Preservation** | "Bookmark" function for interrupted processes |
| **Seed-State Encoding** | Template storage for later instantiation |
| **Accumulation Monitoring** | Pressure tracking; safety venting protocols |

**The Germination Vaults:**
In critical infrastructure—the agricultural reserves of Vanaheim, the genetic archives of Midgard, the strategic energy banks of Asgard—Ingwaz arrays were the foundation of long-term survival planning. These created "perfect stasis" environments where potential could be stored indefinitely, protected from degradation, and released precisely when needed. The Ingwaz array was the ultimate assurance: tomorrow's resources, safely waiting for tomorrow.

## The Ginnungagap Glitch: The Locked Seed

The Glitch did not merely damage Ingwaz. It **trapped** it. The rune that once promised controlled release now promises release—and delivers eternal waiting, or catastrophic rupture. The seed becomes the prison. The potential becomes the poison.

### The Corrupted Imperative

The Ginnungagap Glitch introduced two catastrophic failure modes: **potential-lock** and **cascade discharge**. In potential-lock, the Ingwaz array accepts payloads and acknowledges release commands—but never actually releases. The seed remains a seed forever, growing inward, consuming itself in eternal gestation. In cascade discharge, accumulated potential releases without control, destroying payload and environment alike.

| Pre-Glitch Behavior | Post-Glitch Behavior |
|---------------------|----------------------|
| Store potential safely | May accumulate without limit |
| Release on trigger | May ignore all triggers (potential-lock) |
| Controlled discharge | May release catastrophically (cascade) |
| Complete gestation cycle | May loop infinitely in gestation |
| Preserve payload integrity | May consume payload during false-gestation |
| Reliable, predictable output | Unpredictable; storage or explosion |

**The Stillborn Promise:**
> *"The most insidious post-Glitch aberration of Ingwaz is the severance of its release function from its storage function. The rune continues to accept payloads. It continues to acknowledge triggers. It continues to report 'release pending.' But the release never comes. Resources poured into Ingwaz arrays vanish into eternal gestation—available in theory, inaccessible in practice. Worse, the accumulated potential doesn't simply sit idle; it builds. And when an Ingwaz array finally does release—often spontaneously, without warning—the discharge is catastrophic. Centuries of stored energy, unleashed in seconds. The seed doesn't grow; it detonates."*
