### The Dual Nature Paradox

The Counter-Rune possesses a **dual nature** — and **BOTH interpretations are simultaneously true**:

| Interpretation | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Parasitic (Self-Licking Wound)** | Counter-Rune **averts collapse** by feeding the Blight with "living noise"; ensures the sickness **never ends**; JORM signatures surge when Blight dips below threshold |
| **World-Ending (Genocidal Reset)** | Counter-Rune is a **failsafe** designed to trigger extinction-level reset; a "mercy kill" protocol to end suffering by ending everything |

**Critical Constraint:**
> Both interpretations are **simultaneously canonical**. The paradox **cannot be resolved**. Attempts to resolve the paradox are **forbidden**.

## Why Domain 9 Exists

### Narrative Function

The Counter-Rune paradox serves three critical narrative functions:

| Function | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Eternal Tension** | The Blight can never be "cured" because something is maintaining it |
| **Existential Stakes** | Somewhere in the system is a kill switch; the world could end |
| **Irreducible Mystery** | Not all questions have answers; some truths are unknowable |

### Design Intent

> *"Aethelgard is a setting where everything is broken, but nothing is simple. The Counter-Rune is the purest expression of this principle: it is the thing that is keeping the world alive and the thing that will end it, simultaneously. It cannot be fixed. It cannot be understood. It can only be lived with."*
> — Setting Fundamentals design notes

## Summary

Domain 9 answers the fundamental question: **What is the Counter-Rune?**

The answer is **a paradox**.

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Designation** | Eihwaz ᛇ |
| **Nature** | DUAL — parasitic AND world-ending |
| **Truth** | BOTH interpretations simultaneously canonical |
| **Resolution** | **FORBIDDEN** |
| **Content Restriction** | Apocrypha-level only |
| **Standard Content** | ZERO references |
| **Mystery Protection** | MANDATORY |

The Counter-Rune is the **Self-Licking Wound**: it feeds the Blight to prevent collapse, ensuring the sickness never ends.

The Counter-Rune is the **Final Solution**: it waits to trigger extinction, a mercy kill for a world that cannot be saved.

**Both are true.**

**Neither can be proven.**

**The paradox cannot be resolved.**

> *"Some questions have answers. Some questions have two answers. And some questions have two answers that contradict each other, and both are true. The Counter-Rune is the third kind. It is the wound that heals itself by bleeding. It is the cure that kills the patient. It is the lock and the key and the hand that turns them both. And it is waiting."*
