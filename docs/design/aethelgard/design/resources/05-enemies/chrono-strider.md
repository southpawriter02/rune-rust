## Summary

The Chrono-Strider is causality made predator.

It was designed to be the ultimate scout—perceptive, evasive, tireless, and absolutely untouchable within its temporal domain. Its chrono-ocular array was engineered to see futures. Its phase-shift locomotion was built to occupy optimal frames. Its biology was crafted to thrive in temporal chaos.

And it works. All of it works. **Perfectly.**

That is the tragedy.

The Glitch did not corrupt the Chrono-Strider's core systems. It did not shatter its perception or break its locomotion. The creature's bio-temporal sensors emerged from the catastrophe enhanced—flooded with infinite probability data, adapted to existence across multiple frames simultaneously.

The only thing that was lost was the filter.

The discrimination protocols. The handler commands. The operational parameters that told it what to hunt and when to stop.

Now the filter is saturated.

And so the Strider does what it was designed to do. It perceives threats. It calculates trajectories. It selects the frame where the attack has already missed.

And then it strikes.

The blow cannot be blocked. It lands where you will be, not where you are. Your sword swings through empty air where the creature was a heartbeat ago—or will be a heartbeat from now.

There is no hitting a Chrono-Strider through skill or speed or luck.

There is only chaos.

There is only randomness.

There is only abandoning every predictable pattern and hoping the dice fall in your favor.

Because if you move with rhythm, you are already dead.

The creature has seen your future.

And in every future it can perceive, your timing was perfect—and perfectly useless.

The hunt across time has never, ever ended.
