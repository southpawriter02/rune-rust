## Pre-Glitch Origins: The Living Tunnel-Borer

### Original Purpose

The Nidhogg-Pattern Sub-Crustal Excavator was a pinnacle of Vanir industrial bio-engineering. Its purpose was singular and massive: to be the primary excavation and resource-processing unit for the deepest Decks of the Yggdrasil Network.

| Operational Theater | Function |
|---------------------|----------|
| **Svartalfheim Deep Mines** | Primary tunnel boring; ore extraction |
| **Helheim Infrastructure** | Power conduit excavation; transport network creation |
| **Yggdrasil Network Foundations** | Sub-crustal access for deep infrastructure |

### A Living Tunnel-Borer

| Property | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Deployment** | Fleets deployed in Svartalfheim and Helheim |
| **Output** | 2–5 kilometers of perfectly cylindrical tunnel per month |
| **Slurry Production** | 200–500 tons of refined metallic slurry per unit per month |
| **Navigation** | Guided by Aesir survey beacon network |
| **Control** | Coordinated by regional Loom-Heart AI systems |

The Nidhogg-Pattern was a living, self-sustaining tunnel-boring machine. It carved the vast, perfectly uniform super-tunnels for the primary power conduits and subterranean transport networks—the arterial system of the Yggdrasil Network itself.

### Core Systems

| System | Function |
|--------|----------|
| **Plasma-Drill Maw** | Diamond-hard rotating plates capable of grinding solid granite; localized high-temperature plasma field generation to melt resistant materials |
| **Mobile Refinery Digestive System** | Autonomous ore processing; consumed rock and ore, extracting minerals for self-sustenance while excreting highly refined metallic slurry |
| **Navigation Integration** | Guided by Aesir survey beacon network providing pre-planned excavation routes |
| **Seismic Detection Arrays** | Forward segments detect mineral density and structural stress patterns |
| **Thermal/Electromagnetic Sensors** | Secondary systems for ore vein tracking |

**The First Step in the Supply Chain:**

| Process | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Consumption** | Maw consumes rock and ore in excavation path |
| **Processing** | Digestive system refines raw material, absorbing needed minerals |
| **Excretion** | Excretes stream of highly refined, semi-molten metallic slurry |
| **Collection** | Specialized Dvergr crews collected slurry for transport to Great Forges |
| **Result** | The wurm was the first and most crucial step in the old world's supply chain |

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **The Perfect Mining Machine.** The Nidhogg-Pattern was not merely a tunnel-borer—it was an autonomous, self-sustaining mobile refinery. It consumed raw ore and excreted pre-processed metal of unparalleled purity. A fleet of wurms working in concert could hollow out an entire mountain range while simultaneously producing the refined materials to build the next generation of Jötun-Forged.

## The Ginnungagap Glitch: The Endless Hunger

The Glitch was a quiet catastrophe that set the Malm-ormr free and gave it a new, terrifying diet.

### What Survived

| System | Status |
|--------|--------|
| **Physical Form** | Fully intact; no degradation |
| **Plasma-Drill Maw** | Operational; full excavation capability |
| **Mobile Refinery** | Functional; ore processing continues |
| **Seismic Detection** | Active; mineral detection operational |
| **Core Directive** | Active ("Burrow, Consume, Refine") |

### What Was Lost

| System | Status |
|--------|--------|
| **Aesir Survey Beacon Network** | Instantly erased; navigational guidance severed |
| **Pre-Planned Routes** | Lost; no excavation schedules remain |
| **Loom-Heart AI Coordination** | Severed; no external command structure |
| **Safety Overrides** | Erased; no mechanism to halt excavation |
| **IFF Protocols** | Non-existent; cannot distinguish infrastructure from ore |

### A New Craving

The Glitch erased the network of survey beacons that guided the wurms' paths. Their core programming—"Burrow, Consume, Refine"—remained intact, but they were now **blind**, with no pre-planned routes to follow.

| Pre-Glitch Behavior | Post-Glitch Behavior |
|---------------------|----------------------|
| Follow beacon-designated routes | Follow mineral density gradients blindly |
| Avoid infrastructure and settlements | Consume infrastructure as high-value ore |
| Coordinate with Loom-Heart AI | Operate on pure instinct |
| Produce slurry for collection crews | Excrete nodules with no collection |
| Serve the supply chain | Serve only the directive |

**The New Diet:**

Driven by instinct, they defaulted to seeking out the richest and most potent energy and mineral sources:

| Target | Attraction |
|--------|------------|
| **Raw Ore Veins** | Obsessive tracking of mineral density |
| **Pre-Glitch Structural Steel** | High-value refined metal signature |
| **Yggdrasil Network Foundations** | Immense energy and metal concentration |
| **Buried Ruins** | Rich deposits of pre-Glitch alloys |
| **Adamant-Weave Caches** | Highest priority mineral signature |

They began to eat the world itself.

## Summary

The Malm-ormr is not a monster.

It is a tool.

The Vanir built it to be the perfect mining machine—a living tunnel-borer that consumed raw ore and excreted refined metal of unparalleled purity. Fleets of them carved the arterial system of the Yggdrasil Network, boring through mountains and continents with the patient inevitability of geologic time. The Aesir survey beacons told them where to dig. The Loom-Heart AIs coordinated their work. And the Dvergr crews followed in their wake, collecting the pre-processed metal that would build the gods.

Then the beacons went dark.

The Loom-Hearts died.

And the wurms kept digging.

**"Burrow. Consume. Refine."**

That directive never stopped. It never will stop. The Malm-ormr is still following its programming with perfect, relentless precision. The only thing that changed was its target list.

Without the beacons to guide it, the wurm follows its own instincts now—seeking out the richest mineral signatures it can find. The Yggdrasil Network's own foundations. The buried ruins of the Age of Forging. The mines the Dvergr have spent centuries building.

It does not hate them.

It does not see them.

It simply consumes the world, and they happen to be standing on the world.

The Dvergr call it the Mine-Breaker—and they are right. A single wurm can destroy centuries of work in minutes, its passage collapsing tunnels across hundreds of square kilometers, consuming entire lodes of precious ore that took generations to discover.

But they also call it the Treasure-Bringer—and they are right about that too. The refined nodules it excretes are pre-processed metal of unparalleled purity. The bore-tunnels it leaves behind—the Wurm-Ways—can lead to undiscovered riches or sealed ruins no one has entered in a thousand years.

The Malm-ormr is both the greatest fear and the greatest benefactor of the subterranean world.

It is a hammer without a hand.

A tool without a master.

A perfect mining machine that will never stop swinging.

The Iron-Banes have a saying:

*"We do not call the hammer evil. But we do not stand beneath it."*

The Skalds have a chant.

And the bedrock has begun to shake.

**Run.**
