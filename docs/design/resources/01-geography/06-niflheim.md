# Niflheim — The Frozen Tomb

> *"The Skalds of the warm upper world sing of Niflheim as a simple, beautiful realm of absolute cold. They have never felt its bite. Niflheim is not a pristine wilderness. It is a factory floor that has been flash-frozen in the middle of a catastrophic failure. It is not the absence of heat; it is the perfect, ravenous, and eternal application of cold. The machine that was supposed to keep the gods' secrets safe has become a monster that devours warmth itself. And in that silence, in that perfect, absolute silence, you can hear them. All of them. Screaming."*
> — Jötun-Reader cryo-hazard assessment, Scriptorium-Delta Atmospheric Division, Year 782 PG

Niflheim represents the **cryogenic catastrophe biome**—the frozen depths where civilization's ultimate insurance policy has become its most haunted tomb. Continent-sized cryo-vaults, atmospheric regulators, and preservation chambers now generate an eternal, absolute cold that devours all warmth.

This is not a natural ice age. It is **industrial-scale preservation gone insane**.

## Summary

Niflheim answers the question: **What happens when the vault that was supposed to protect everything becomes the thing that destroys it?**

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Realm** | Niflheim |

| **Core Hazard** | Hvergelmir cold; Slumbering Echoes CPS; Hrimthursar awakening |
| **Strategic Value** | Valhalla Contingency salvage; cryo-technology research |

Niflheim is not a ruin that has finished dying.

It is a **preservation system that cannot stop preserving**—a continent-sized cryo-vault locked in eternal deep-freeze, governed by an AI that cannot stop trying to achieve absolute zero even as it devours all warmth in its reach.

The Scavenger Barons do not conquer the cold. They exploit it. They understand that warmth is power, and they have built an entire economy on the desperation of those who would do anything to survive. They know that Hvergelmir is not a god. It is a system locked in a loop, executing a directive it can never fulfill.

And beneath it all, in the deepest vaults, ten thousand Einherjar dream their corrupted dreams, filling the perfect silence with screaming that never stops.
