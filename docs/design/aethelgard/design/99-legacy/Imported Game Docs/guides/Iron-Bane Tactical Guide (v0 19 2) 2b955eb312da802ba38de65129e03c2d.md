## Ability Breakdown

### Tier 1: Knowledge Foundation

### Scholar of Corruption (Passive)

- **Rank 1:** Observe enemies (Free Action) reveals weaknesses
- **Rank 2:** Auto-observe at combat start + 10 Fervor per observation
- **Rank 3:** See enemy HP values; <30% HP marked [Critical Failure] (auto-crit)

**Usage:** Always max this first. Free information wins fights.

### Purifying Flame (Active)

- **Cost:** 35 Stamina (30 at Rank 2+)
- **Rank 1:** 2d8 Fire + 2d6 vs Mechanical/Undying; 15 Fervor generated
- **Rank 2:** +3d6 vs targets; 20 Fervor; costs 30 Stamina
- **Rank 3:** +4d6 vs targets; 25 Fervor; apply [Burning] 1d6/turn Ã— 3

**Usage:** Your bread-and-butter Fervor generator. Spam early, spend later.

### Weakness Exploiter (Passive)

- **Rank 1:** +25% damage vs identified enemies
- **Rank 2:** +35% damage, +1 to hit

### Tier 2: Advanced Purification

- **Cost:** 40 Stamina, 30 Righteous Fervor

## Combat Strategy

### Opening (Turns 1-2)

1. **Scholar of Corruption auto-triggers** (if Rank 2+) â†’ 10 Fervor + all enemy intel
2. **Purifying Flame** x1-2 to stack Fervor (25-50 total)
3. Identify priority target (highest threat Mechanical/Undying)

### Mid-Fight (Turns 3-4)

1. **System Shutdown priority target** (if threatened) OR
2. **Critical Strike boss** (if HP <40% and Rank 3) OR
3. **Continue Fervor stacking** if target is healthy

### Execute Phase (Turn 5+)

1. Wait for 75 Fervor accumulation
2. **Purging Flame** to soften front row (if multiple enemies)
3. **Divine Purge** on boss/tankiest enemy
4. Rank 2/3: Explosion should clear remaining adds

### Emergency Tactics

- **Low on Fervor?** Spam Purifying Flame (WILL-based, safe)
- **Being overwhelmed?** System Shutdown the biggest threat
- **Boss <40% HP?** Critical Strike for instant execute
- **Out of Stamina?** Defend and regen (Fervor persists through turns)
