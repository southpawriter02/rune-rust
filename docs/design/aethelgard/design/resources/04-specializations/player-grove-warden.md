# The Grove-Warden — The Uncorrupted Code

> *"Grow where ground is good, endure where it is not. The world's sickness contains no cure—only the living patterns of uncorrupted earth can overwrite the Blight's madness."*
> — Grove-Warden creed

---

## Overview

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Archetype** | Mystic |
| **Role** | Nature-Based Controller / Purifying Healer / Versatile Shapeshifter |
| **Path Type** | Coherent |
| **Primary Attribute** | WILL |
| **Secondary Attribute** | STURDINESS |
| **Resource System** | Aether Pool (AP) |
| **Trauma Economy Risk** | Low (Innate resistance to Corruption; power from uncorrupted sources) |

The **Grove-Warden** is the Mystic specialization that embodies the philosophy that the world's uncorrupted source code can be channeled as a weapon against the Blight.

They are the protectors of the last bastions of pure, untainted life—mystics who draw power from the ancient, vibrant, and logical energies of the natural world. Their magic is one of life, growth, and primal fury, a direct and violent rejection of the paradoxical, anti-life nature of the Runic Blight.

They are the **antibody of a sick world**. Where the Blight creates paradox and static, the Grove-Warden fosters logical growth and harmony. Where the Blight infects, the Grove-Warden purifies.

To choose the Grove-Warden is to embrace the fantasy of the classic druid and the versatile shapeshifter. Your saga is a constant, zealous war against the encroaching corruption, a story of nurturing the last green shoots in a world of rust and ruin.

### The Antibody of a Sick World

| **Growth Against Decay** | Life is the antithesis of the Blight's entropy |

The Grove-Warden represents the antithesis of the Blight itself. Where the Rust-Witch accelerates entropy and the Blight-Weaver channels chaos, the Grove-Warden channels the **world's immune system**.

> *"The Blight is a virus in the world's source code. I am the antivirus. My magic does not merely heal wounds—it overwrites corrupted code with the logic of life. When I call upon the roots to bind a Forlorn, I am not casting a spell; I am imposing tangible, logical reality upon a being that should not exist."*

This is not nature-worship as the old world understood it. This is **system restoration**—the act of accessing and replicating the world's original, uncorrupted patterns.

## Gameplay Fantasy

### Player Experience

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Core Fantasy** | Classic druid; versatile shapeshifter; primal healer; nature's champion |
| **Gameplay Feel** | Heal → Control → Transform → Adapt |
| **Success Metric** | Party sustained; enemies rooted; forms shifted to meet needs |
| **Decision Space** | When to heal vs. control vs. transform; environmental awareness |

Playing a Grove-Warden is about **versatility and adaptation**.

Your gameplay loop is:
1. **Assess**: Read the environment and enemy composition
2. **Heal**: Mend wounds and purge corruption from allies
3. **Control**: Entangle and restrain enemies with living roots
4. **Transform**: Shift into bestial forms when the situation demands
5. **Purify**: Cleanse the Blight's influence from allies and enemies alike

> *"One turn, I am a back-row healer, mending the Warrior's wounds with Nature's Balm. The next, I transform into Bear Form and hold the line while the tank recovers. The turn after that, I shift to Wolf Form and tear into the exposed flank. The Grove-Warden does not have one role—we have all of them."*

You are the party's **adaptable anchor**—capable of filling the role of healer, controller, tank, or damage-dealer as the needs of battle change. This makes you one of the most flexible specializations in the game.

### Environmental Attunement

| Environment | Effect |
|-------------|--------|
| **\[Natural\]** (Vanaheim, Midgard forests) | AP regeneration increased; spell potency amplified |
| **\[Mechanical\]** (Jötunheim depths) | AP costs increased; spell effectiveness reduced |
| **\[Corrupted\]** (Helheim, Blight zones) | AP costs increased; connection to pure Aether stifled |

The Grove-Warden's power is intrinsically linked to their environment. This is not a weakness—it is **resonance**.

> *"In the forests of Vanaheim, I am twice the mystic I am in the iron halls of Jötunheim. This is not a flaw; it is the nature of my power. I do not command the Aether—I harmonize with it. And harmony requires something pure to harmonize with."*

## Core Mechanic: Bestial Transformation

### The Art of the Wild

| Form | Role | Primary Stat | Key Ability |
|------|------|--------------|-------------|
| **Human Form** | Healer / Controller | WILL | Spellcasting (Nature's Balm, Entangling Roots) |
| **Bear Form** | Tank / Bruiser | STURDINESS | \[Maul\] melee attack, \[Roar\] taunt |
| **Wolf Form** | Damage / Scout | FINESSE | \[Savage Bite\] with \[Bleeding\], enhanced senses |

Bestial Transformation is the core of the Grove-Warden's versatility. You can expend AP to shapeshift into different animal forms, each with its own stats and abilities.

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Activation** | Standard Action |
| **Cost** | 50-55 AP (varies by form) |
| **Duration** | 3 turns |
| **Effect** | Your stats are temporarily replaced by the form's stats |
| **Limitation** | Cannot cast spells while transformed |

> *"When I take on the Aspect of the Bear, I am not merely wearing a bear's skin. I am channeling the unyielding, indomitable spirit of the great Rune-Bear—an avatar of the world's uncorrupted life force. My flesh shifts. My bones reform. And for three turns, I AM the bear."*

### Transformation Philosophy

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Form Follows Need** | Transform only when the situation demands it |
| **Commitment** | Transformation is total—you cannot cast spells while shifted |
| **Timing** | A wrong form at the wrong time kills crews |

The Grove-Warden's transformations are not casual shapeshifts—they are **primal system restores**. You momentarily overwrite your own biological code with the uncorrupted pattern of a great beast.
