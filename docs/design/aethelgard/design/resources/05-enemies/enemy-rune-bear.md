# Rune-Bear — Corrupted Bio-Aetheric Guardian Specification

> *"The Vanir created a perfect vessel to hold the purest energy. Now the energy is poison, and the vessel is in agony. The Rune-Bear does not hate you. It does not even see you. It is a star that has collapsed in on itself, and it is simply raging against the pain of its own, unending implosion."*
> — Lead Ecologist's Note, Scriptorium-Primus

The Rune-Bear is a legendary monster—one of the most powerful and unpredictable creatures in the wilds of Aethelgard. It is not a spirit or a demon, but a **living reactor**: a noble guardian of a forgotten age driven mad by a diet of pure poison.

> [!DANGER]
> **Apex Predator Classification.** The Rune-Bear is responsible for 8–15 civilian deaths annually. Encounter doctrine is **AVOID-WHEN-POSSIBLE**. Engagement only authorized when territorial conflict threatens civilian routes or strategic salvage operations.

The Runic Blight saturated the Aether, transforming the creature's fuel source into a perpetual poison.

| Stage | Effect |

| Situation | Minimum Distance |
|-----------|------------------|

### Detection Indicators

**Visual:**
- Characteristic heavy forward-lean silhouette
- Blue-white glow from runic scars in low light

- Territorial claw marks on trees/wrecks (8–12 cm gouges at 1.8–2.2 m height)

**Acoustic:**
- 40–60 Hz chest hum indicates bear within 200 m
- Pulsing to 80 Hz indicates agitation state

**Olfactory:**
- Distinctive musky-ozone odor stronger near den sites
- Air tastes of ozone; hair stands on end

### Field Card: Rune-Bear Proximity Protocol

| Phase | Protocol |

| **DURING** | Monitor approach; abort if retreat radius <40 m despite deterrence; use bell cadence at 85 dB every 30 seconds if bear continues approach |

## Summary

The Rune-Bear is tragedy made flesh—a noble guardian engineered to protect and stabilize, now condemned to an existence of unending agony. It does not hunt because it is hungry. It does not rage because it is evil. It attacks because every moment of its existence is pain, and the only response its corrupted mind can produce is violence.

The Skalds call it a spirit of the forest. The God-Sleepers call it holy. The Iron-Banes call it a demon.

They are all wrong.

The Rune-Bear is a sick, miserable creature in a constant state of agonizing pain. And when it sees you, it is going to do its level best to share that pain.

The glowing runes in its fur are not armor. They are not divine markings. They are cancerous tumors—the Runic Blight trying to write itself onto living tissue, failing, and causing agony with every pulse of light.

Do not meet its charge. You cannot block a Rune-Bear.

Do not stand your ground. It is a siege engine wrapped in fur.

Be the tree, not the stone. Dodge. Use the environment. Make it crash. Make it stumble.

And if you must fight—aim for the brightest runes. They are the source of its power.

They are also the source of its pain.

Strike true, and the beast will scream.

And in that moment of shared agony, you might survive.
