## Pre-Glitch Origins: The Endless Spring

### Original Function

In the Age of Forging, Berkano (ᛒ) served as the root function for biological repair, regeneration, and pattern-accurate tissue restoration. Where Uruz (ᚢ) provided raw vitality and metabolic acceleration, Berkano provided the *template*—the biological blueprint that ensured regeneration produced correct tissue in correct configurations.

| Operational Theater | Function |
|---------------------|----------|
| **Medical Systems** | Accelerated wound closure; organ repair; limb regeneration |
| **Bio-Synthetic Maintenance** | Pattern-accurate restoration of bio-mechanical components |
| **Agricultural Management** | Controlled crop growth; yield optimization |
| **Environmental Restoration** | Ecosystem repair; biomass reclamation |
| **Longevity Protocols** | Cellular senescence reversal; age-damage repair |

### The Template Principle

Berkano's distinctive characteristic was its operation through pattern-recognition rather than simple growth acceleration:

| System | Function |
|--------|----------|
| **Pattern-Lock** | Reads original biological template; constrains growth to that pattern |
| **Saturation Recognition** | Identifies when regeneration is complete; terminates growth |
| **Differentiation Control** | Ensures correct tissue types form in correct locations |
| **Integration Verification** | Confirms new tissue properly interfaces with existing structures |
| **Metabolic Regulation** | Controls resource consumption during regeneration |
| **Boundary Enforcement** | Prevents growth beyond damaged area |

**The Healing Gardens:**

In the great medical facilities of Asgard—the regeneration chambers of the Aesir elite, the bio-synthetic repair bays of the Einherjar legions—Berkano arrays were the foundation of all biological restoration. A severed limb could be regrown in days, perfectly matching the original. A failing organ could be rebuilt cell by cell, the Berkano protocol reading the body's own template and executing flawless reconstruction. The Aesir had conquered death-by-injury. Berkano was the rune of "grow back, grow right, grow *only* what was lost."

The Glitch did not merely damage Berkano. It **corrupted** it. The rune that once knew when to stop no longer recognizes completion. The healing that once followed templates now writes its own. The growth that once restored now devours.

### The Corrupted Imperative

The Ginnungagap Glitch introduced a catastrophic failure mode: **pattern-lock failure**. Instead of regenerating according to original biological templates, Berkano can lose its reference point and enter a state of **purposeless proliferation**—growth that no longer serves restoration, only itself.

| Pre-Glitch Behavior | Post-Glitch Behavior |
|---------------------|----------------------|
| Regenerate damaged tissue | May regenerate indefinitely |
| Follow original template | May abandon template; create novel forms |
| Stop at wound boundaries | May exceed boundaries; consume surrounding tissue |
| Produce correct tissue types | May produce wrong types; undifferentiated masses |
| Use supplemented resources | May consume patient's own healthy tissue |
| Recognize completion | May never recognize saturation point |

**The Devouring Bloom:**

> *"The most insidious post-Glitch aberration of Berkano is the loss of saturation recognition. The regenerating tissue does not know it is finished. It continues growing past the wound margin, consuming adjacent healthy tissue for metabolic fuel. The growth is not 'cancerous' in the pathological sense—it is 'healthy' tissue, pink and vital and alive. It simply does not stop. It does not differentiate appropriately. It does not recognize boundaries. The patient is not killed by disease; they are killed by healing. They are devoured by their own restoration, consumed by flesh that was meant to save them."*
