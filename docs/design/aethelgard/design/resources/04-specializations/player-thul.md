# The Thul — The Saga-Breaker

> *"Find the keystone belief. Remove it. Let the structure fall."*
> — Thul doctrine

---

## Overview

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Archetype** | Adept |
| **Role** | Controller/Debuffer, Support |
| **Path Type** | Coherent |
| **Primary Attribute** | WITS |
| **Secondary Attribute** | WILL |
| **Resource System** | Stamina + Action Points (AP) |
| **Trauma Economy Risk** | Medium |

The **Thul** is the cold, calculating mind of a broken world.

They are not the inspirational poets of the Skalds. They are the **grim sages**—cynical orators and masters of psychological deconstruction who have learned that in the Age of Echoes, **a broken narrative is a weapon**.

Where a Skald builds a heroic saga to inspire, a Thul dissects an enemy's saga to expose its flaws, its fears, and its logical fallacies, causing a **catastrophic collapse in morale**.

They counsel not for glory but from grim necessity: **when the enemy's keystone belief crumbles, the entire structure falls**.

Their precision is their weapon against chaos. Where the Berserkr charges screaming into the fray and the Skald rallies with song, the Thul waits. They observe. They analyze. They speak with certainty. And then the enemy's will shatters—because every saga has a flaw.

---

## Core Philosophy: The Deconstruction of Narrative

### The Three Principles

| Principle | Expression |
|-----------|------------|
| **Observe Before Speaking** | Read the target's stance, history, and tells before committing rhetoric |
| **Find the Keystone** | Every will has a single belief that holds it together—find it |
| **Let the Structure Fall** | Remove the keystone cleanly; the collapse follows naturally |

The Thul's creed is not written in saga or scripture. It is written in the **language of flaw**—hesitation, pride, fear, old failures, and the subtle tells of a mind that can be broken.

> *"A Jarl is a sun. His will gives warmth and life to his Hold. His rage is a fire that can burn it to the ground. A Thul is his shadow. We do not command; we counsel. We do not fight; we reflect."*

This is not the rhetoric of the old sagas—no boasting of victories, no glory in the crushing. The Thul understands that in the Age of Echoes, **every leader has a flaw**, and that flaw is a weapon waiting to be wielded.

They take only the words that end the threat. They record stance, tell, weakness, and outcome.

Reproducibility and predictability win battles before they begin.

### The Coherent Path

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Source** | Rhetoric and analysis, not heresy |
| **Method** | Patient observation, surgical verbal strikes |
| **Cost** | Moderate Psychic Stress from Saga of the Broken Will (deep deconstruction is taxing) |
| **Philosophy** | The enemy's narrative is a weakness to exploit, not a power to embrace |

Unlike the Rust-Witch who channels entropy or the Myrk-gengr who wraps themselves in corruption, the Thul maintains **coherent discipline**. They study the mind without losing their own. They exploit psychology without accumulating Blight.

This is a sustainable path—demanding, but not self-destructive.

## Gameplay Fantasy

### Player Experience

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Core Fantasy** | Battlefield chess master; the voice that ends the war |
| **Gameplay Feel** | Analyze → debuff → enable → repeat |
| **Success Metric** | Priority threats neutralized; allies empowered to execute |
| **Decision Space** | Target prioritization, debuff sequencing, buff timing |

Playing a Thul is about **reading the battlefield** and making surgical psychological strikes.

You identify the highest-priority intelligent enemy—the chieftain, the alpha, the rival spellcaster—and you analyze it. From your position, you deliver devastating rhetoric that strips away confidence, coherence, and the will to fight. Your Saga of the Broken Will can shatter an enemy leader so completely that their damage output halves and they become vulnerable to all damage types.

> *"You are the cold, calculating mind of a broken world. You've learned to read the invisible flaws in every narrative—the pride, the fear, the old failures that reveal a creature's psychological weak points. You identify high-priority threats, expose their weaknesses, and enable your allies to execute with certainty."*

Your power scales with your enemy's intelligence. The smarter the target, the more devastating your rhetoric. This makes you the ideal specialist for facing intelligent enemies—the leaders, tacticians, and spellcasters that others struggle to bring down.

## Abilities: The Orator's Arsenal

### Tier 1 — Foundational Rhetoric

| Ability | Type | Cost | Effect |
|---------|------|------|--------|
| **Keeper of Sagas I** | Passive | — | +1d10 to Rhetoric checks and investigate checks for creature lore/weaknesses |
| **The Sage's Insight** | Active | 30 Stamina | WITS check; reveals one Resistance + one Vulnerability; applies \[Analyzed\] for 2 rounds |
| **Inspiring Word** | Active | 35 Stamina | Single ally gains +1d10 to next attack's Accuracy Pool for 2 rounds |

**Keeper of Sagas I** represents the Thul's vast mental library of oral histories, laws, and—most importantly—the records of past failures and fatal flaws. They know the stories that end badly, and they know why.

At higher ranks, this passive allows identification of psychological vulnerabilities and automatic detection of leadership structures within enemy groups—critical for prioritizing targets.

**The Sage's Insight** is the core analysis ability—a focused examination that reveals enemy weaknesses AND marks them for the party. This establishes the Thul's identity as the battlefield's intelligence officer.

> *"You focus your analytical mind on a single foe, observing their stance and tactics to identify a critical flaw in their strategy. The weight of their own weakness becomes visible to all."*

**Inspiring Word** is the Thul's precision buff. Unlike the Skald's emotional appeal, the Thul appeals to logic and tactical purpose—a short, powerful word that reminds an ally of their exact role in the plan.

### Tier 2 — Advanced Oration

| Ability | Type | Cost | Effect |
|---------|------|------|--------|
| **Demoralizing Diatribe** | Active | 45 Stamina | WILL + Rhetoric vs WILL; inflicts \[Disoriented\] for 2 rounds |
| **Logic Trap** | Active | 50 Stamina | WITS + Rhetoric vs WITS; inflicts \[Stunned\] for 1 round |
| **Resolute Presence** | Passive | — | Allies in same row gain +1 die to WILL Resolve Checks vs \[Fear\] |

**Demoralizing Diatribe** is the Thul's signature psychological attack—a barrage of scathing logic and recounted failures that shatters enemy confidence.

This is not a magical curse or a simple insult. It is a surgical, psychological assault. The Thul recounts past failures of the target's clan with perfect clarity, exposes the logical flaws in their position, or simply speaks the truth they cannot bear to hear.

> *"You unleash a barrage of scathing, logical insults—recounting a past failure of your foe's clan with perfect clarity. Their confidence shatters under the weight of undeniable truth."*

**Logic Trap** is the Thul's hard control ability—a paradoxical question or deeply flawed logical proposition that overloads an enemy's mind, causing them to freeze in confusion.

> *"You present an opponent with a paradoxical question—a deeply flawed proposition that their mind struggles to process. Thought stalls. Action ceases."*

**Resolute Presence** rewards proper positioning. The Thul's calm, analytical presence is a coherent signal in the psychic static—a source of stability that helps nearby allies resist fear.

### Tier 3 — Mastery of the Mind

| Ability | Type | Cost | Effect |
|---------|------|------|--------|
| **Keeper of Oaths** | Active | 55 Stamina | Single ally gains \[Inspired\] (+2 damage dice) + Fear Immunity for 3 rounds |
| **The Unspoken Truth** | Passive | — | Demoralizing Diatribe upgrade: Critical Success also inflicts \[Feared\] |

**Keeper of Oaths** is the Thul's premier ally buff—a powerful invocation of ancient oaths that grants \[Inspired\] AND Fear immunity.

| Effect | Duration |
|--------|----------|
| \[Inspired\] | +2 damage dice for 3 rounds |
| Fear Immunity | Complete immunity to \[Fear\] for 3 rounds |

> *"You invoke a powerful, ancient oath, reminding an ally of their sworn duties and filling them with an unshakeable sense of purpose. The oath binds. The fear breaks."*

**The Unspoken Truth** represents the moment the Thul's insight transcends mere analysis. Some truths are better left unspoken. The Thul speaks them anyway.

| Demoralizing Diatribe Result | Effect |
|------------------------------|--------|
| Success | \[Disoriented\] for 2 rounds |
| Critical Success | \[Disoriented\] + \[Feared\] for 2 rounds |

> *"Your insight is now so profound that you can perceive the deep-seated fears and insecurities of your opponents—and weaponize them with surgical precision."*

### Capstone — Saga of the Broken Will

| Component | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **Cost** | 70 Stamina, once per combat |
| **Check** | WITS + Rhetoric vs WILL (massive bonus) |
| **Success** | \[Will Broken\] for 3 rounds: damage output halved, Vulnerable to all damage types |
| **Psychic Cost** | Large Psychic Stress on the Thul |

The capstone **Saga of the Broken Will** represents the ultimate expression of the Thul philosophy—absolute mastery of psychological deconstruction.

The Thul does not recite an inspiring saga. They recite a **devastating one**: the perfectly logical, step-by-step prediction of their target's inevitable defeat. The argument is so flawless, so undeniable, that it shatters the enemy's will to exist.

> *"You speak not of glory but of ending. You lay out the logical, step-by-step prediction of your target's inevitable defeat—their flawed tactics, their doomed position, their certain death. The argument is so perfect, so undeniable, that their will to exist simply... breaks."*

Against a priority target, Saga of the Broken Will transforms the battlefield. Combined with The Sage's Insight's \[Analyzed\] debuff and Demoralizing Diatribe's \[Disoriented\], this creates a devastating combo: the enemy leader becomes half as effective, vulnerable to everything, and perfectly positioned for your allies to execute.

## Systemic Integration

### Intelligent Target Exploitation

| Enemy Intelligence | Thul Advantage |
|--------------------|----------------|
| **Mindless** | No effect (Beasts, most Undying) |
| **Low** | Basic debuffs function; reduced effectiveness |
| **Medium** | Full debuff effectiveness; standard durations |
| **High** | Enhanced debuff durations; bonus to opposed checks |
| **Genius** | Maximum effectiveness; Saga of the Broken Will gains additional bonus |

The Thul's power scales with enemy intelligence—the smarter the target, the more devastating the rhetoric. This creates a unique niche: **the anti-leader specialist** who excels precisely where other builds struggle.

Against intelligent enemies—the chieftains, alphas, and rival spellcasters who coordinate enemy forces—the Thul's psychological debuffs provide devastating control.

**Critical Limitation:** The Thul's entire toolkit is almost useless against non-sentient Blighted Beasts or mindless Undying. This creates a clear and compelling weakness that must be covered by party members.

### Trauma Economy Interaction

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Path Type** | Coherent (no negative Trauma interactions) |
| **Self-Inflicted Risk** | Medium (Saga of the Broken Will costs large Psychic Stress) |
| **Mitigation** | High WILL score essential for personal defense |
| **Strategic Implication** | Sustainable support; can operate for extended encounters without spiral |

The Thul is a **Coherent** specialization. Their power comes from rhetoric, observation, and discipline—not from heretical bargains with corruption.

The Saga of the Broken Will Psychic Stress cost is significant but manageable. A high WILL score is essential for the Thul's own defense, as their powerful analytical abilities require them to interface with the world's madness.

### Party Synergies

| Ally | Synergy |
|------|---------|
| **Jötun-Reader** | Intelligence partner; JR identifies, Thul dismantles |
| **Damage Dealers** | \[Analyzed\] + \[Will Broken\] = massive damage amplification |
| **Tanks / Front Line** | Resolute Presence grants Fear resistance; Keeper of Oaths prevents breaks |
| **Controllers** | Debuff stacking; Logic Trap's \[Stunned\] enables follow-up control |
| **Bone-Setter** | Manages Psychic Stress from Saga of the Broken Will |

The Thul is the **battlefield's intelligence officer and force multiplier**—identifying the highest-value threat and enabling the party to execute with surgical precision.

> *"With Jötun-Reader: they identify the anatomy, you dismantle the mind. With damage dealers: you create the opening, they deliver the killing blow."*

### Positional Considerations

| Position | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| **Same Row as Allies** | Resolute Presence active; +1 die to Fear resistance for nearby allies |
| **Different Row** | Resolute Presence inactive; reduced party support |
| **Near Intelligent Enemy** | Optimal for Demoralizing Diatribe and Logic Trap |

The Thul is optimized for **mid-line operation**—close enough to support allies with Resolute Presence, positioned to deliver rhetoric against priority targets.
