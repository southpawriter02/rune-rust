## Core Identity

**Improvised Trap** uses scavenged wire and sinew to create simple but effective snares. Not elegant—pragmatic. **Crude, but it works.**

---

## Mechanics

### Cost

15 Stamina | **Components:** 1× [Scrap Metal] + 1× [Tough Leather]

### Effect

- Place trap on tile within 2 tiles
- First enemy stepping on trap: [Rooted] (1 turn)
- Multiple traps can be placed

### Rank Progression

| Rank | PP Cost | Effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | 3 | [Rooted] 1 turn |
| 2 | 20 | [Rooted] 2 turns, place up to 3 tiles away |
| 3 | 35 | [Rooted] 2 turns + [Bleeding] (2d4/turn, 3 turns) via Master Improviser |

---

## Tactical Applications

**Pre-Combat Setup:**

- Place traps at chokepoints, flanking routes, retreat paths
- Lock enemies in kill zones for ranged attackers

**Synergies:**

- **Ranged attackers:** Lock enemies in place for free shots
- **Master Improviser:** Doubles effectiveness (auto-Rank 3)
- **Area denial:** Force suboptimal enemy routing

## Failure Modes

<aside>
⚠️

**Component Dependency** — Ability blocked without [Scrap Metal] + [Tough Leather]. Maintain 5× component reserve.

</aside>

<aside>
⚠️

**Action Economy** — Crafting requires Standard Action. Pre-combat placement preferred over reactive mid-combat use.

</aside>

<aside>
⚠️

**Target Limitations** — Ground-based only. Flying/teleporting enemies bypass entirely. Single-target (first trigger only).

</aside>

<aside>
⚠️

**Detection Risk** — Intelligent enemies (WITS 5+) may spot crude traps (Perception vs DC 14).

</aside>
