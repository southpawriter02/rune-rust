## Pre-Glitch Origins: The Perfect Herd

### Original Purpose

The ancestor of the Slag-Horned Aurochs was a common but vital Vanir bio-asset: a genetically optimized and highly resilient breed of cattle designed to feed a civilization.

| Operational Theater | Function |
|---------------------|----------|
| **Midgard Habitation Decks** | Primary protein source for civilian populations |
| **Curated Park Ecosystems** | Free-range grazing on managed grasslands |
| **Arcology Food Systems** | Self-replicating protein production |

These were not designed for combat. Their purpose was **absolute, peaceful efficiency**—the living solution to the problem of feeding a perfect world.

### The Living Protein Factory

| Property | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Body Plan** | Genetically optimized cattle; hardy, fast-growing, efficient |
| **Temperament** | Docile herd behavior; responsive to handlers |
| **Growth Rate** | Engineered for rapid maturation and high protein yield |
| **Grazing Efficiency** | Optimized to thrive on curated Midgard grasses |
| **Herd Behavior** | Passive; predictable; easy to manage |

Unlike predatory bio-assets, the Bos-Taurus-Primus was designed to be a simple, elegant, and self-replicating solution. They were a living symbol of the era's peace and prosperity.

### A Symbol of Peace

| Property | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Environment** | Managed pastures; curated park-like ecosystems |
| **Diet** | Engineered grasses; optimized feed efficiency |
| **Behavior** | Placid; easily handled; predictable grazing patterns |
| **Cultural Role** | Common sight on Midgard plains; testament to Vanir abundance |

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **The Grazing Herds.** The Bos-Taurus-Primus was everywhere in the Age of Forging—a placid, common sight on the engineered plains. They were the quiet, unnoticed foundation of Midgard's food security. Nobody thought about them. They were simply *there*, grazing peacefully in the curated gardens of paradise.

## The Ginnungagap Glitch: The Feral Stampede

The Glitch turned the perfect protein source into the perfect battering ram.

### What Survived

| System | Status |
|--------|--------|
| **Genetic Resilience** | Fully functional; enhanced by survival pressure |
| **Robust Constitution** | Operational; adapted for harsh environments |
| **Herd Instincts** | Preserved; repurposed for defensive coordination |
| **Size Potential** | Unlocked; no longer constrained by design parameters |

### What Was Corrupted

| System | Status |
|--------|--------|
| **Handler Management** | Severed instantaneously |
| **Containment Systems** | Failed; herds stampeded into ruins |
| **Docile Temperament** | Replaced by territorial aggression |
| **Size Regulation** | Removed; survival pressure selected for maximum size |
| **Grazing Territory** | Shifted from curated parks to industrial wasteland |

### The Liberation

The failure of arcology containment systems allowed the vast herds to escape their managed pastures and **stampede into a world that was no longer peaceful**.

> **"A New, Harsh World."** The once-lush plains were now littered with the wreckage of the "Civil War of Glitches." The Aurochs' environment was now a wasteland fringe—a dangerous borderland between the overgrown fields of Midgard and the industrial graveyards of Jötunheim.

**Survival of the Aggressive:** New predators like the Ash-Vargr relentlessly hunted the herds. Over generations, only the largest, strongest, and most aggressive individuals survived to pass on their genes. The docile creature of the old world was **bred out of existence**, replaced by a savage, territorial behemoth.

**Forged in the Ruins:** The most significant adaptation was behavioral. The Aurochs learned to frequent industrial ruins and slag heaps, using their horns to root through debris in search of vital salt and mineral deposits. This constant, abrasive interaction with super-heated and twisted metal had a **dramatic effect on their horns**.

## Summary

The Slag-Horned Aurochs is peace turned to fury.

It was designed to be the perfect protein source—docile, efficient, self-replicating, grazing peacefully in the curated parks of a perfect world. Its robust constitution was engineered for reliable growth. Its gentle temperament was optimized for easy handling. Its predictable behavior made it the foundation of Midgard's food security.

And it worked. All of it worked. **Perfectly.**

That is the irony.

The Glitch did not corrupt the Aurochs' core systems. It did not break its resilience or weaken its constitution. The creature's genetic optimization emerged from the catastrophe intact—but the world it was built to graze had become a graveyard of twisted steel and cooling slag.

The only things that were lost were the handlers.

The managed pastures. The curated grasses. The containment fields.

Now the cow was free. And the wolves were hungry.

So the prey did what prey does when there are no shepherds. It grew **large**. It grew **strong**. It grew **mean**.

Generation after generation, the weak died in the jaws of the Ash-Vargr. The docile died under the hooves of the aggressive. The small died in the shadow of the massive.

And the survivors learned.

They learned that the slag heaps held the minerals they needed. They learned that rooting through twisted metal **forged their horns into weapons**. They learned that the fire that killed other creatures could not touch their thickened hides.

They learned that the broken world was not a prison but an **armory**.

And now they thunder across the Slag Wastes—the peaceful cows that became battering rams. The protein stock that became siege engines. The gentle grazers that will charge anything that moves and **dare you to try and take them back**.

The Vanir engineered a perfect cow to live in a perfect garden.

The garden is gone.

The cow remains.

And it wears the armor of our failure on its head.

**It is a more honest creature than its creators ever were.**
