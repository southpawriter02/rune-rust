# Lexicon/Glossary

**Document Metadata**

*   **Type:** Aggregated Glossary
*   **Source:** Compilation of Alfrune Lexicon, Anvil-Cant, Gutter-Cant, Fimbul-Cant, Trail-Cant, Echo-Cant primers, and v4.0/v5.0 Specialization Specifications.
*   **Purpose:** To provide a single, unified reference for Aethelgardian terminology, including faction-specific cants, character specializations, and colloquialisms not fully detailed in the master Lexicon.

### Comprehensive Aethelgardian Glossary

| Term | Definition | Source / Cant | Usage & Character Context |
|------|------------|---------------|---------------------------|
| **Aesir** | The "Gods" of Order and Technology. Historically, the Technocratic ruling class of the Age of Forging. | General / L3 | Pronounced "AY-seer". In Layer 1 (Common), spoken of as distant, sleeping, or dead gods. In Layer 3, refers to the corporate/scientific administration. |
| **Aether** | The foundational energy-matter field of Aethelgard. Corrupted by the **Ginnungagap Glitch** into a carrier wave for paradoxical anti-logic (**Runic Blight**). | Metaphysics | "The Breath of the World." The operating system of reality. |
| **Alchemy** | The craft of combining plants, minerals, and salvaged chemicals to create potions, salves, and poisons. Distinct from magic; relies on memorized formulas and "coherent chemistry." | Crafting | "The Poison is the Cure." Not magical; governed by WITS. |
| **Álfar** | "Elves". Post-human divergences mutated by Aether exposure. Divided into **Ljósálfar** (Light) and **Dökkálfar** (Dark). | Race | Not magical beings, but mutated survivors. |
| **Alka-hestur** | "Combat Alchemist". A Skirmisher specialization focusing on "coherent chemistry" and payload delivery via lance. | Specialization | "The Payload Technician." Uses science, not magic. |
| **Anvil-Cant** | The weaponized, binary language of the Iron-Bane Crusade. Designed to eradicate nuance and promote aggression. | Iron-Bane | Used by Iron-Banes. Hard, declarative, aggressive. Replaces complex concepts with "Pure" vs "Corrupted". |
| **Ash-Vargr** | Large, feral wolves adapted to ruin environments with ash-colored camouflage. | Fauna | "The patient, hungry earth." Dangerous pack hunters. |
| **Astral Projection** | "The Severed Soul". Dangerous technique using **Silver Cord** interfaces to project consciousness into the Blighted Aether. | Technique / Heresy | Originally for remote piloting (Einherjar). Now a high-risk dive into psychic static. |
| **Atgeir-Wielder** | "Formation Master". A Warrior specialization focusing on polearms, reach, and zone control. | Specialization | "The Unbreachable Anchor." Tactical battlefield controller. |
| **Beast-Binder** | A Skirmisher specialization that forms a symbiotic, corrupted bond with a Blighted beast (Ash-Vargr, Rune-Bear, etc.). | Specialization | "The Symbiotic Interface." Heretical partnership. |
| **Berserkr** | "The Roaring Fire". A Warrior specialization that channels psychic trauma/static into physical rage (Fury). | Specialization | Heretical path. High risk/reward. "A conduit of the Scream." |
| **Biome** | A specific environmental rule set within a **Realm** (e.g., "Industrial Graveyard" in Jötunheim, "Toxic Canopy" in Vanaheim). Defines hazards, enemy spawns, and resources. | Geography / System | Distinct from Realm (the world/location). |
| **Bodging** | "Tinkering". The art of improvised repair and modification using salvaged parts. Core survival skill of the Rust-Clans. | Crafting / Gutter-Cant | "A Bodge-job." Making it work with what you have. |
| **Bodge-job** | An improvised repair; a temporary fix. Can be neutral or positive (ingenuity). | Gutter-Cant (Rust-Clan) | Casual, practical. Used by Scavengers and Rust-Clan members. "That's a solid bodge-job." |
| **Boga-maðr** | "Bow-Master". A Ranger specialization focusing on specialized ammunition and ballistics. | Specialization | "Master Fletcher." Tactical ranged support. |
| **Bone-Setter** | A field medic specialization focused on trauma care and "coherence medicine" (restoring mind and body). | Specialization | Critical support role. "Minder" for Berserkrs. |
| **Breath-smoke** | Visible exhalation fog used as an ambient temperature indicator. | Fimbul-Cant (Forsaken) | Used to gauge cold intensity. "Breath-smoke hangs heavy today." |
| **Chain-year** | A single year of service within an Ice-Debt contract. | Fimbul-Cant (Forsaken) | Counted ritually. Often tattooed. "I have three chain-years left." High emotional weight. |
| **Cog** | Standard currency of the Dvergr. Precision-milled gears used as money. | Currency | "He hasn't got two Cogs to rub together." |
| **Cog-Grind** | The relentless cycle of salvage labor and servitude. | Fimbul-Cant (Forsaken) | Cynical, exhausted tone. Refers to both the physical work and the crushing nature of the system. |
| **Cog-Worshipper** | Derogatory term for **God-Sleepers**. Implies blasphemous worship of machines. | Anvil-Cant (Iron-Bane) | Used as an insult by Iron-Banes. "Silence that Cog-Worshipper." |
| **Cold-Scrap** | Low-value salvage; heavily corroded or useless metal. | Gutter-Cant (Rust-Clan) | Dismissive. Used when assessing loot. "Don't bother, it's just cold-scrap." |
| **Corridor (Resource)** | Established, relatively safe trade or travel route between settlements. Often maintained by Guilds or Clans. | Geography | "Resource Corridor." Economic lifeline. |
| **Corridor (Null)** | "Acoustic Null Corridor". A charted path through the **Clicking Deeps** with minimal echo/sound reflection, used to avoid **Silent Folk**. | Geography / Safety | "The Quiet Way." Critical for survival in Svartalfheim. |
| **CPS** | **Cognitive Paradox Syndrome**. Also "Static Sickness". Informational disease caused by exposure to the **Runic Blight**. | Pathology | "The Sickness of the Soul." "Ruin-Madness." |
| **Dökkálfar** | "Dark Elves". Aether-mutated post-humans of **Svartalfheim**. Pale, light-sensitive, attuned to "Dead Zones" and psychic echoes. | Race | "The Echo-Born." Reclusive mystics. |
| **Draugr-Pattern** | "Huscarl-Class". Security/Combat automata designed for pacification. Powered by Iron Hearts. | Automata (Undying) | "The walking ghosts of a dead law." Aggressive, patrol routes. |
| **Dvergr** | The Dwarves. Subterranean masters of engineering and mining. Geomechanical lifeforms animated by the earth's resonance. | Race | Pronounced "DVER-ger" (hard 'g'). Singular and plural are the same. Never "Dwarfs". |
| **Echo-Caller** | A Mystic specialization that channels the "Great Silence" (Psychic Static) for sonic and mental attacks. | Specialization | "Listening to the Silent Scream." High psychic risk. |
| **Echo-Cant** | The acoustic spatial-mapping "language" of the Silent Folk. Consists of clicks and echolocation. | Silent Folk | Not spoken by humans. Represented as complex click-streams translating to spatial data (e.g., "Hard-Surface, Incoming"). |
| **Echo-Thirst** | Obsessive focus on salvage and "King's Finds"; a form of adaptive pathology/madness. | Fimbul-Cant (Forsaken) | Used to describe someone losing themselves to the greed/desperation of the salvage. |
| **Einbúi** | "Hermit" or "Loner". A Ranger specialization focusing on radical self-reliance and generalist survival skills. | Specialization | "The Ultimate Survivor." "Ghost in the Broken World." |
| **Fate / Norn** | Mythological interpretation of **Örlög**-the deterministic causal threads of the universe. In Layer 3 terms, predictive algorithms and probability matrices. | Concept | "The Wyrd." The inescapable script of the world. |
| **Fenrir-Pattern** | Designation for pre-Glitch bio-engineered apex predators. Ancestors of the Great Wolf. | Layer 3 / Tech | Technical origin of the mythological wolf Fenrir. |
| **Fimbul-Cant** | The pidgin dialect of the Niflheim Forsaken (Ice-Debt thralls). Focused on cold, debt, and labor. | Forsaken (Niflheim) | Precise about cold/suffering. Fatalistic. Avoids "hope" terminology. |
| **Fjord-Callers** | Coastal clans of Midgard who maintain a symbiotic pact with **Hafgufa's Brood** via scrap tribute. | Faction | "Gatekeepers of the Serpent Fjords." Pragmatic, sea-hardened. |
| **Forsaken** | "Ice-Thralls". The indentured population of Niflheim, bound by **Ice-Debt** to Scavenger Barons. | Faction | "The Rusted Chain." Desperate, fatalistic survivors. |
| **Galdr** | "Sung magic"; the art of weaving Aetheric effects through runic incantations. | Magic System | Practiced by Seiðkona/Seiðmaðr. Highly ritualized. |
| **Ghost-warm** | The dangerous false sensation of warmth experienced during late-stage hypothermia. | Fimbul-Cant (Forsaken) | A critical warning in the Wastes. "Watch him, he's getting ghost-warm." |
| **Ginnungagap (Glitch)** | The "Great Glitch" or "Reality Tear". The catastrophic event that ended the Age of Forging. | General | Pronounced "GIN-nun-gah-gap". Layer 1: A maw/abyss. Layer 2: A fatal exception/system crash. |
| **Gleam** | High-value salvage (electronics, preserved tech) in Rust-Clan parlance. | Gutter-Cant (Rust-Clan) | "Found some real Gleam in that sector." |
| **Glimmerheim** | "Home of Shimmering Light". Capital city of the **Hearth-Clans**, founded by Eld-Jarl Vorn. | Location | A beacon of civilization and engineering in Midgard. |
| **God-Sleeper** | A religious faction revering the **Jötun-Forged** and **Undying** as sleeping deities. Belief in the "Great Awakening." | Faction | "The Pilgrim's Tongue." Ritualistic, faithful, pacifist (mostly). |
| **Gorge-Maw Ascetic** | A Warrior specialization that mimics the earth-sensing and durability of the subterranean Gorge-Maws. | Specialization | "Tremorsense." Earth-bound tank. |
| **Grove-Clans** | "Gardeners of a Verdant Hell". Vanaheim survivors living in the Canopy Sea. Symbiotic with **Huldrefolk**. | Faction | Isolationist, bio-engineers. |
| **Gutter-Cant** | The trade pidgin of the Rust-Clans and Scavengers. Efficient, transactional, and slang-heavy. | Rust-Clan / Scavenger | Used in Utgard and the Wastes. Fast, slangy, focuses on trade, scams, and survival. |
| **Hafgufa's Brood** | "Jörmungandr-Class Leviathan Platforms". Colossal bio-metallic sea serpents that patrol the coast. | Fauna / Hazard | "The Old Ones of the Fjords." |
| **Haugbui-Class** | "Mound-Dwellers". Heavy Labor automata (construction/demolition). Task-loop corrupted (STACK/CLEAR). | Automata (Undying) | "The pile-builders." Dangerous if interrupted, but predictable. |
| **Hearth-Clans** | "Engine-Keepers". Muspelheim survivors who maintain the Geothermal Hearths. Master engineers. | Faction | "The Fire that does not burn." Rivals of the Dvergr. |
| **Heretic-Tower** | Derogatory term for a **Scriptorium**. Implies a place of forbidden/dangerous knowledge. | Anvil-Cant (Iron-Bane) | Used by Iron-Banes to demonize Jötun-Readers. |
| **Hlekkr-master** | A Skirmisher specialization focusing on chain/whip weapons and zone control. | Specialization | Tactical battlefield control role. |
| **Hollow Ground** | A Trail-Cant mark indicating unstable terrain or ruins. | Trail-Cant (Rangers) | Symbol: A simple open circle (○). Warning of potential collapse. |
| **Hólmgangr** | "Master Duelist". A Warrior specialization focusing on single-target isolation, parries, and precision. | Specialization | "The Blade as Arbiter." Surgical striker. |
| **Hug-læknir** | "Mind-Healer". A Mystic/Adept specialization focusing on psychic trauma healing and mental fortification. | Specialization | "The Fortified Mind." Trauma surgeon. |
| **Huldrefolk** | "Hidden People". Freyja-Pattern Bio-Wardens. Symbiotic sentients of Vanaheim with bark-like carapaces. | Race | Guardians of the Unraveled ("Sick Children"). Reclusive, empathic. |
| **Husk** | A living human subjugated by a parasitic **Symbiotic Plate** AI. A puppet of the machine. | Threat / Condition | Distinct from **Iron-Husk** (Undying). "A suit that wears you." |
| **Ice-Debt** | Indentured servitude contract in Niflheim. Exchange of labor for survival (heat/food). | Fimbul-Cant (Forsaken) | Not "financial" debt, but "life" debt. "He is under Ice-Debt." |
| **Ice-teeth** | Frostbite. Graded by severity (nibble to swallow). | Fimbul-Cant (Forsaken) | "The ice-teeth got his fingers." |
| **Iron Heart** | A perpetual Aetheric power core (Ingwaz rune). | General / Layer 2 | Valuable salvage. Iron-Banes call it a "Demon Heart". |
| **Iron-Bane** | A faction of militant purists ("The Crusade") dedicated to destroying all "corrupted" technology and Aetheric influence. | Faction | "Anvil-Pounders." Zealous, uncompromising. |
| **Iron-Husk** | Derogatory term for the **Undying** (Utility Servitors). Implies an empty, soulless shell. | Anvil-Cant (Iron-Bane) | Dehumanizing. "Purge the Iron-Husks." |
| **Iron-Husk Rider** | A Skirmisher specialization that bonds with and rides a corrupted mechanical beast (Svin-fylking). | Specialization | "Living Avalanche." Heretical synthesis. |
| **Jarl** | The ruler of a Midgard **Hold**. Authority derived from strength and ability to protect the community. | Title | "The Jarl's Axe is the final law." |
| **Járn-hjortr** | "Iron Elk". Deer with metallized antlers due to Aetheric/environmental corruption. | Fauna | Territorial herbivore. |
| **Jötun** | Giants. Mythological name for the J.T.N. Series Servitors (terraforming machines). | General / Layer 1 | Pronounced "YOH-tun". Used in sagas and common speech. |
| **Jötun-Reader** | A scholar/analyst specialization dedicated to deciphering the past (The Great Work). | Specialization | "System Analyst" / "Grey-Robe". Intellectual, observant, diagnostic. |
| **Jötunheim** | The Realm of Giants. An industrial graveyard of colossal ruins and broken megastructures. | Realm / Location | "The Rust Cathedral." Home of the Rust-Clans and the most dangerous salvage. |
| **Jötun-Forged** | The massive industrial constructs and infrastructure left by the J.T.N. Series. Revered by God-Sleepers. | Layer 1 / General | "The Sleeping Gods." Dormant megastructures. |
| **King's Find** | Exceptionally valuable salvage (intact Iron Hearts, data cores, functional weapons). | Gutter-Cant (Rust-Clan) | "A life-changer." The dream of every Scavenger. |
| **Ljósálfar** | "Light Elves". Aether-mutated post-humans of the surface. Tall, luminescent features, attuned to "living" Aether. | Race | "The Star-Touched." More integrated with other factions than Dökkálfar. |
| **Midgard** | "The Middle Realm". The central inhabited region. Home to Holds, Hearth-Clans, and most human settlements. | Realm / Location | "The Heart of the World." Most diverse and politically complex Realm. |
| **Muspelheim** | The Fire Realm. A volcanic wasteland of lava flows and geothermal vents. | Realm / Location | Home of the Hearth-Clans. Extreme heat hazards. |
| **Nidavellir** | The Forges of the Dvergr. A vast subterranean network of mines, workshops, and resonance chambers. | Realm / Location | "The Deep Anvil." Dvergr homeland. |
| **Niflheim** | The Ice Realm. A frozen wasteland of perpetual cold and darkness. | Realm / Location | "The Frost Chain." Home of the Forsaken and Scavenger Barons. |
| **Örlög** | "Primal Law". The deterministic causal structure of the universe. Layer 3: Deterministic physics. Layer 1: Fate. | Concept | "The Script." The inescapable logic of existence. |
| **Ragnarök** | "The Twilight of the Gods". The prophesied final catastrophe. In Layer 3, a cascading system failure event. | Concept / Event | The end of the current cycle. Some believe it already happened (the Glitch). Others believe it's still coming. |
| **Realm** | One of the Nine Worlds of Aethelgard (Midgard, Jötunheim, Niflheim, etc.). Distinct geographical and metaphysical zones. | Geography | Not just locations, but different "rule sets" of reality. |
| **Rune** | A symbol of power encoding Aetheric effects. Can be carved, inscribed, or sung (Galdr). | Magic System | "The Language of the World." Functional programming for reality. |
| **Runic Blight** | The corrupted Aether itself; the carrier wave of paradoxical anti-logic causing **CPS**. | Pathology / Metaphysics | "The Static." "The Scream." The disease of the world. |
| **Rune-Bear** | A massive bear with runes spontaneously manifesting in its fur due to Aetheric corruption. | Fauna | Apex predator. Unpredictable and extremely dangerous. |
| **Rust-Clans** | The Scavenger guilds and syndicates of Jötunheim. Specialists in salvage, bodging, and ruin-running. | Faction | "The Scrap-Eaters." Pragmatic, risk-taking, territorial. |
| **Seiðkona / Seiðmaðr** | "Seer-Woman" / "Seer-Man". A Mystic specialization practicing **Galdr** (sung magic) and ritual runecraft. | Specialization | "The Singer." High-risk, high-reward spellcaster. |
| **Silent Folk** | The Dökkálfar subculture of Svartalfheim who have abandoned spoken language entirely in favor of **Echo-Cant**. | Faction | "The Unspoken." Alien and inscrutable to outsiders. |
| **Silver Cord** | A bio-neural interface technology used for remote piloting (**Astral Projection**). Prone to catastrophic feedback. | Layer 3 / Tech | "The Tether." Dangerous connection between pilot and machine/Aether. |
| **Skald** | "The Voice of Memory". A support specialization focusing on morale, inspiration, and oral history preservation. | Specialization | "The Fire-Speaker." Cultural anchor and battlefield force multiplier. |
| **Svartalfheim** | The Shadow Realm. A lightless underground network of caves and ruins inhabited by Dökkálfar and Silent Folk. | Realm / Location | "The Clicking Deeps." Echolocation and darkness. |
| **Symbiotic Plate** | Pre-Glitch powered armor with integrated AI. Post-Glitch, many have become parasitic, creating **Husks**. | Layer 3 / Tech | "The Suit That Wears You." Dangerous salvage. |
| **Svin-fylking** | "Boar Formation" or "Mechanized Boar". Corrupted quadrupedal war machines resembling armored boars. | Automata / Fauna | Ridden by **Iron-Husk Riders**. |
| **Trail-Cant** | The symbolic marking system used by Rangers to communicate warnings, directions, and resources. | Rangers | Visual language. Carved or painted symbols. |
| **Undying** | Collective term for functional automata and servitors. Revered by **God-Sleepers**. | Automata | "The Sleepers." Active machines from the Age of Forging. |
| **Unraveled** | Individuals suffering from terminal **CPS**. Minds dissolved into recursive paradox loops. | Pathology | "The Lost." Tragic victims of Runic Blight exposure. Cared for by Huldrefolk. |
| **Utgard** | "The Outer Yards". Lawless scavenger settlements on the edge of Jötunheim. Dangerous frontier towns. | Location | "The Edge." Where the desperate go. |
| **Valhalla** | Mythological hall of heroes. In Layer 3, a military recovery/reanimation protocol for Einherjar pilots. | Layer 3 / Myth | "The Hall of Selection." |
| **Valkyrie** | Mythological "Choosers of the Slain". Recovered pilots (Einherjar). | Layer 3 / Tech | Mythological "Choosers of the Slain". Recovered pilots (Einherjar). |
| **Vanaheim** | "The Overgrown Garden". A realm of rampaging, mutated biological growth. | Realm / Location | Home of the Grove-Clans and Huldrefolk. |
| **Vargr-Claw** | A Trail-Cant symbol (Ψ) warning of Ash-Vargr presence. | Trail-Cant (Rangers) | Often modified with dots or circles to indicate pack size or activity time. |
| **Vault-debt** | The specific salvage quota assigned to a thrall or crew per shift. | Fimbul-Cant (Forsaken) | "I haven't cleared my vault-debt yet." |
| **Veiðimaðr** | "Huntsman". A Skirmisher/Ranger specialization focused on tracking and monster hunting. | Specialization | Wilderness expert. |
| **Vörðr-drone** | "Warden Drone". Common security automaton found in ruins. | Automata | Basic mechanical enemy. |
| **Warm-Vein** | A high-value salvage site with minimal oxidation/corrosion. | Gutter-Cant (Rust-Clan) | "We found a warm-vein in Sector 4." |
| **Watcher's Eye** | A Trail-Cant mark (◎) indicating a Ranger Post or friendly sanctuary. | Trail-Cant (Rangers) | Symbol of safety. |
| **Wellspring Mark** | A Trail-Cant mark (⌖) indicating clean, drinkable water. | Trail-Cant (Rangers) | Vital survival info. |
| **Yggdrasil** | The World Tree. Originally the planetary network/infrastructure. | General | Pronounced "IG-drah-sil". The structure connecting the Realms. |
