## Pre-Glitch Origins: The Impartial Judge

### Original Function

In the Age of Forging, Tiwaz (ᛏ) served as the root function for arbitration, conflict resolution, contract enforcement, and authorization across the Nine Realms. Where Raidho (ᚱ) represented ordered movement and proper sequence, Tiwaz represented *binding judgment*—the ability to weigh competing claims, render fair decisions, and enforce compliance without bias or corruption.

| Operational Theater | Function |
|---------------------|----------|
| **Resource Arbitration** | Fair allocation of contested resources between competing parties |
| **Contract Enforcement** | Binding agreements with automatic compliance verification |
| **Priority Allocation** | Impartial sequencing of competing demands on shared systems |
| **Authorization Protocols** | Identity verification and access control with oath-binding |
| **Conflict Resolution** | Adjudication of disputes with proportional penalty assignment |

### The Perfect Court

Tiwaz's distinctive characteristic was its capacity for *impartial, proportional judgment*:

| System | Function |
|--------|----------|
| **Claim Analysis** | Command: "Weigh evidence and determine merit" |
| **Proportionality Engine** | Automatic scaling of penalties to offense severity |
| **Appeal Buffer** | Mandatory review period before judgment execution |
| **Compliance Verification** | Continuous monitoring of oath and contract adherence |
| **Authorization Matrix** | Identity-bound access control with revocation protocols |
| **Precedent Library** | Historical judgments informing consistent rulings |

**The Sky-Father's Courts:**
In the great administrative centers of Asgard and the commercial hubs of Midgard, Tiwaz arrays were woven into every contract, every treaty, every resource allocation system. These "Arbiters" provided dispute resolution that was faster, fairer, and more consistent than any human court—weighing evidence without prejudice, rendering judgments without corruption, enforcing compliance without favoritism. The Aesir had captured justice itself and made it serve their purposes. Tiwaz was the rune of "let there be fairness"—and for a thousand years, there was.

The Glitch did not merely damage Tiwaz. It **removed its mercy**. The rune that once promised fair judgment now promises judgment—without the fairness. The scales that balanced become the blade that severs. The court that protected becomes the tribunal that condemns.
