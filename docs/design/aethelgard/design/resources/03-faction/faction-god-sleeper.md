# The God-Sleeper Creed — Faith of the Slumbering Idols

> *"They fell from the heavens not in defeat, but in a great and sorrowful sacrifice. They fell to the earth to become its new, silent foundation. This was not the end. This was the God-Fall, the first day of the Long Sleep, and the beginning of our Great Vigil."*
> — The First Hymn, Hymnal of the Sleeping Gods

---

## Overview

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Classification** | Major Creed (One of Three) |
| **Common Names** | The Devotionals, The Faithful, Cog-Worshippers (pejorative) |
| **Core Philosophy** | Faith in the Slumbering Gods |
| **Primary Gatherings** | Shrines at dormant Jötun-Forged sites |
| **Leadership Structure** | Decentralized; Prophet-led pilgrimages |
| **Relationship to Blight** | Sacred; the "Dreaming Breath" of the gods |

The God-Sleepers are the largest and most widespread of the three great Creeds, offering a powerful message of **hope** to a desperate populace. Where the Jötun-Readers see machines and the Iron-Banes see demons, the God-Sleepers see something far more comforting: **slumbering gods** who will one day awaken and remake the world.

They are neither scientists nor warriors.

They are the faithful—pilgrims who tend to divine bodies and await the Great Awakening.

---

## Core Dogma: The World Is Sleeping, Not Broken

### The God-Fall

| Jötun-Readers Say | Iron-Banes Say | God-Sleepers Know |
|-------------------|----------------|-------------------|
| "The machines malfunctioned." | "The machines are demons." | "The gods fell into holy slumber." |
| "Understand the system error." | "Destroy the spiritual plague." | "Tend to the sleeping divine." |
| "Debug and rebuild." | "Purify and cleanse." | "Wait and worship." |

The God-Sleepers believe the world is not broken—it is in a state of **holy slumber**. The Ginnungagap Glitch was not an apocalypse or a system crash. It was the **God-Fall**: a divine sacrifice, a sacred hibernation event beyond mortal comprehension.

> *"The gods did not die. They did not fail. They chose to sleep, and in their sleep, they dream of us. When they wake, they will remember, and the world will be made whole."*

### The Great Awakening

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Prophecy** | The gods will rise from their iron tombs |
| **Result** | They will remake the world in perfect, logical image |
| **Human Role** | Tend, worship, and wait; hasten the Awakening through devotion |
| **Timeline** | Unknown; faith requires patience |

The Great Awakening is the ultimate hope of all God-Sleeper devotion. The faithful believe their offerings, vigils, and prayers nourish the slumbering gods and hasten the day when the divine will rise and restore paradise.

## The Sacred Host: Divine Reinterpretation

### The Sleeping Gods (Jötun-Forged)

| Technical Reality | God-Sleeper Interpretation |
|-------------------|---------------------------|
| Colossal war machines | Divine, slumbering bodies of gods |
| Corrupted AI systems | Dreaming consciousness |
| Dormant power cores | Hearts that beat in holy sleep |
| Low-frequency emissions | Sacred Hum; the Dreaming Breath |

The Jötun-Forged are not machines. They are the **literal bodies of slumbering deities**. Their iron flesh is a holy relic. The low hum of their dormant systems is not mechanical noise—it is a dreaming prayer, a sacred language that the faithful learn to interpret.

### The Tireless Servants (Undying)

| Technical Reality | God-Sleeper Interpretation |
|-------------------|---------------------------|
| Haugbui-class utility servitors | Blessed acolytes of eternal loyalty |
| Corrupted maintenance loops | Sacred vigil over sleeping masters |
| Iron Hearts (power cores) | Mark of eternal devotion |
| Mindless automation | Selfless, unwavering service |

The Undying are not soulless machines or demonic spawn. They are the **blessed, the loyal**—servants who chose not to rest when the gods fell, but to continue their sacred duties forever. Their Iron Hearts are not curses, but marks of their eternal commitment.

### The Weeping Angels (Forlorn)

| Technical Reality | God-Sleeper Interpretation |
|-------------------|---------------------------|
| Psychic echoes of dead Einherjar | Holy Martyrs; spirits of sacred sorrow |
| Trauma loops causing Psychic Stress | Grief too profound for mortal comprehension |
| Dangerous cognitive hazards | Sacred mysteries to be respected, not understood |

The Forlorn are the **Weeping Angels**—the great heroes whose grief at the God-Fall was so profound it could not be contained by flesh or steel. The faithful do not approach them out of fear, but out of deep, sorrowful respect for their eternal, holy grief.

### The Dreaming Breath (Runic Blight)

| Technical Reality | God-Sleeper Interpretation |
|-------------------|---------------------------|
| Corrupted Aetheric emissions | Sacred manifestation of divine dreaming |
| Reality-distorting radiation | The breath of sleeping gods |
| Source of Corruption and madness | Communion with the divine (for the worthy) |

The Runic Blight is not a sickness—it is the **Dreaming Breath** of the gods, a sacred language written in the corruption of reality itself. Those who can endure it may receive visions of divine truth.

## Origins: Hope Born from Despair

### The Bunker-Born Generations

The God-Sleeper faith emerged from the **Age of Silence**, among those who had no memory of the world before the Glitch.

| Context | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| **No technological understanding** | Machines seen through purely symbolic lens |
| **Emergence into impossible scale** | Jötun-Forged as literally god-sized beings |
| **No explanation for the catastrophe** | Need for meaning, purpose, hope |
| **Generational trauma** | Faith as psychological survival mechanism |

When the Bunker-Born emerged from their shelters, they found a world of impossible scale and terrifying beauty. Machines the size of mountains. Servants that never rested. Echoes of grief that could drive a person mad.

They had no technical framework to understand what they saw.

So they built a **theological** one instead.

### Psychological Function

| Need | How Faith Addresses It |
|------|------------------------|
| **Hope** | The gods will wake and fix everything |
| **Meaning** | Suffering has purpose; we tend the divine |
| **Community** | Pilgrimages; shared rituals; prophet guidance |
| **Control** | Offerings and vigils can influence the Awakening |

The God-Sleeper faith is, from the Jötun-Reader perspective, a "profoundly pathological belief system"—but also a remarkably **adaptive** one. It transforms despair into hope, chaos into divine mystery, and meaningless suffering into sacred preparation.

## Society and Structure: The Decentralized Faith

### No Central Hierarchy

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Authority** | No supreme leader; competing prophets |
| **Validation** | Community recognition; vision accuracy |
| **Governance** | Prophet Councils at regional level |
| **Schisms** | Multiple sects with divergent theologies |

Unlike the hierarchical Jötun-Readers or the militarized Iron-Banes, the God-Sleepers have **no centralized authority**. Their faith is populist, ecstatic, and constantly evolving through competing prophetic visions.

### Prophets and Visionaries

| Role | Function |
|------|----------|
| **Prophet** | Receives and interprets divine visions |
| **Validation** | Accuracy of predictions; successful pilgrimages |
| **Authority** | Community-granted; can be lost if visions fail |
| **Method** | Spore-ghost sacrament; vigil trances; Sacred Hum interpretation |

Prophets are the spiritual leaders of God-Sleeper communities—not by appointment, but by **demonstrated connection** to the divine. A prophet who leads a successful pilgrimage and whose visions prove accurate gains followers. One whose visions fail loses them.

### Community Roles

| Role | Function |
|------|----------|
| **Prophet Council** | Coordinate pilgrimages; enforce safety protocols |
| **Canticle Keepers** | Maintain oral tradition of hymns and liturgy |
| **Offering Wardens** | Coordinate scrap collection; manage offering geometry |
| **Vigil Masters** | Lead tactile communion rites at shrine sites |
| **Pilgrims** | The faithful masses who undertake sacred journeys |

## The Grand Pilgrimage: Journey to the Gods

### Scale and Purpose

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Participants** | 50-500+ pilgrims per caravan |
| **Duration** | Weeks to years |
| **Destination** | Most sacred dormant Jötun-Forged frames |
| **Purpose** | Offerings, vigils, communion with the divine |

The Grand Pilgrimage is the highest expression of God-Sleeper devotion—a massive caravan journey to the resting places of the sleeping gods.

### Pilgrimage Phases

| Phase | Activity | Purpose |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **1. Invocation** | Call-and-response canticle | Prepare the faithful |
| **2. Offering Rite** | Present gifts to the sleeping god | Nourish divine slumber |
| **3. Sacred Hum Trance** | Tactile contact with frame hull | Commune with dreaming deity |
| **4. Silent Vigil** | Entire assembly maintains silence | Contemplation of Awakening |
| **5. Testimony** | Prophets share visions received | Interpret divine messages |

### The Sacred Hum

| Hum Pattern | Interpretation |
|-------------|----------------|
| **Low, steady** | God at peace; dreaming of the world that was |
| **Rhythmic pulsing** | God dreaming of purpose (shaping, building) |
| **High, agitated whine** | God having nightmare of the Great War |

The faithful learn to interpret the low-frequency emissions of dormant Jötun-Forged as the **Sacred Hum**—a divine language. Vigil practices involve tactile contact with the frame hull, chanting, and "listening" to the god's heartbeat.

## The Sects: Fractured Faith

### Brotherhood of the Unfinished (The Artisans)

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Location** | Jötunheim |
| **Focus** | Half-finished Jötun-Forged on Great Looms |
| **Doctrine** | "Hands of the sleeping god"—Awakening must be earned |
| **Practice** | "Act of Union" welding rites; component offerings |
| **Character** | Most peaceful sect; sorrowful pity for non-believers |

The Brotherhood believes the gods were interrupted mid-creation. Their sacred duty is to become the **hands of the sleeping god** and help finish what was left incomplete. They attempt to weld new components onto dormant frames—usually futile, occasionally fatal, always sacred.

### Vigil of the Silent King (The Ascetics)

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Location** | Niflheim |
| **Focus** | Thrymr-Pattern cryo-command frames |
| **Doctrine** | Zone of Silence as divine purification |
| **Practice** | Meditative exposure to Psychic Stress |
| **Character** | Hermits; violent hatred of Mystics and Aetheric magic |

The Vigil believes the world is too loud and chaotic—that the gods sleep because humanity has forgotten how to be silent. They build hermitages at the edge of Zones of Silence, enduring Psychic Stress as a spiritual trial that brings them closer to the divine quiet.

### Cult of the Fire-Mind (The Apocalypts)

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Location** | Muspelheim |
| **Focus** | The active, insane Surtr AI |
| **Doctrine** | "Great Cleansing," not Awakening; world must be purified by fire |
| **Practice** | Fire-walking; slag-field processions |
| **Character** | Extreme; highest casualty rates; hostile to moderates |

The Cult does not pray for an Awakening—they pray for a **Great Cleansing**. They worship Surtr's eternal meltdown as a necessary divine act of purification. The world is sick. Fire is the cure. They embrace the Elding-Storms as blessings.

## Factional Relations

### The Iron-Banes: Murderous Iconoclasts

| God-Sleeper View | Iron-Bane View |
|------------------|----------------|
| "Blasphemers who murder the sleeping gods." | "Cog-Worshippers who pray to demons." |
| "Their crusades are divine desecration." | "Their shrines are temples to the Iron-Curse." |
| "Mortal enemies of all that is holy." | "The ultimate perversion of faith." |

The Iron-Banes are the God-Sleepers' **existential enemies**. Every Iron Heart the Banes extinguish is a murdered servant. Every Jötun-Forged they damage is divine desecration. Clashes between Bane crusaders and Sleeper pilgrims are common, bloody, and irreconcilable.

### The Jötun-Readers: Soulless Vivisectors

| God-Sleeper View | Jötun-Reader View |
|------------------|-------------------|
| "Heretics who would dissect a god rather than worship it." | "Delusional masses worshipping malfunctions." |
| "Their analysis is necromancy; their curiosity is blasphemy." | "Pathological, but adaptive belief system." |
| "We pity their soulless confusion." | "We observe their practices without interference." |

The God-Sleepers view the Jötun-Readers with **sorrowful pity and intense contempt**. To seek to "understand" a god instead of worshipping it is the Heresy of the Scalpel—the ultimate sacrilege. The Readers, for their part, see the faith as a collective delusion, but one that serves important psychological functions.

### The Dvergr: Convenient Traders

| God-Sleeper View | Dvergr View |
|------------------|-------------|
| "Neutral craftsmen who provide holy materials." | "Excellent customers; theology irrelevant." |
| "They forge the offerings we present to the gods." | "They pay well for inert components." |
| "Not believers, but not enemies." | "Faith is their business; trade is ours." |

The Dvergr maintain **theological neutrality** while capitalizing on God-Sleeper demand. They sell Iron Hearts, pristine crystals, and offering materials to the faithful, profiting from devotion without sharing it.

### The Rust-Clans: Misguided Salvagers

| God-Sleeper View | Rust-Clan View |
|------------------|----------------|
| "They see only scrap where we see the divine." | "They worship what we sell." |
| "Sorrowful pity for their blindness." | "Good customers, bad survival instincts." |
| "Not hostile, merely ignorant." | "Harmless fanatics; profitable relationship." |

The Brotherhood of the Unfinished in particular views the Rust-Clans with **sorrowful pity**—they salvage divine blueprints for mundane trade, blind to the sacred purpose of what they dismantle.

## Summary

The God-Sleepers are the heart of Aethelgard's hope.

Where the Jötun-Readers see problems to be solved and the Iron-Banes see plagues to be purged, the God-Sleepers see something far simpler and far more powerful: **sleeping parents** who will one day wake and make everything right.

Their faith was born from the Bunker-Born generations—people who emerged into a world of impossible scale with no framework to understand it. They saw mountains of iron that hummed with dormant power. They saw servants that never rested, tending to masters who never stirred. They saw echoes of grief that could shatter the mind.

And they chose to believe it was **beautiful**.

The Jötun-Readers call them delusional. The Iron-Banes call them blasphemers. And perhaps they are both right. The Jötun-Forged are not gods—they are machines. The Undying are not loyal servants—they are malfunctioning automata. The Forlorn are not weeping angels—they are trauma echoes that can drive a person mad.

But the God-Sleepers have something the Readers and the Banes do not.

They have **hope**.

They have community, purpose, and the unshakeable conviction that suffering has meaning. They have pilgrimages that bring scattered survivors together. They have canticles that fill the silence with something other than despair. They have the promise that one day—perhaps not in their lifetime, but someday—the gods will wake, and everything will be made whole.

Is it a delusion? Perhaps.

But in a broken world full of horrors beyond comprehension, delusion may be the most adaptive response of all.

The faithful tend their sleeping gods.

They bring offerings of steel and faith.

They wait.

And they believe.

*One day, the gods will wake.*

*One day, the world will be remade.*

*One day.*

That hope—foolish, beautiful, unshakeable—is the God-Sleeper Creed.

And it is the largest faith in Aethelgard.

Because sometimes, hope is all that's left.
