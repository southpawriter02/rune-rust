# The Jötun-Forged — Colossal Pilot-Operated Mechanoids

> *"The Jötun-Forged were the pinnacle of Aesir engineering, colossal pilot-operated mechanoids that served as the instruments of their creators' will, capable of building cities, leveling mountains, and waging war on a scale unimaginable to the current inhabitants of Aethelgard. They are not monsters, but the most tragic and dangerous relics of the Age of Forging."*
> — Scriptorium-Primus, Division of Automata Studies

---

## Overview

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Classification** | Colossal Automata (Pilot-Operated) |
| **Scale** | Building to Mountain-sized |
| **Threat Classification** | Boss / World-Threat |
| **Primary Habitat** | Jötunheim (Knuckle-Bone Fields, Great Looms); all Realms |
| **Current State** | Dormant, Corrupted, or Destroyed |

The Jötun-Forged are the god-machines of the Age of Forging—walking titans designed to build and destroy on continental scales. Their corpses form the iron mountains of Jötunheim. Their dormant forms are worshipped as sleeping gods. Their active remnants are extinction-level threats.

> [!DANGER]
> **WORLD-THREAT CLASSIFICATION.** An active Jötun-Forged represents a regional catastrophe. Standard engagement is impossible. Survival requires avoidance, specialized knowledge, or divine intervention. Even dormant frames leak lethal Aetheric radiation and broadcast psychic trauma.

## Pre-Glitch Origins: Gods of Metal and Purpose

### Design Philosophy

The Jötun-Forged were designed for tasks beyond human scale—not as weapons first, but as **instruments of civilization**.

| Class | Purpose |
|-------|---------|
| **Terraforming & Construction** | Planetary engineering, atmospheric regulation, geological shaping |
| **Warfare & Pacification** | Military dominance, strategic deterrence, high-threat neutralization |
| **Special Operations & Command** | Intelligence warfare, command-and-control, prototype development |

They were the hands with which the Aesir shaped worlds.

**The cockpit of a Jötun-Forged is therefore a tomb, and the machine itself is a monument to its pilot's final, agonizing moment.**

## The Ginnungagap Glitch: Walking Tombs

### What Happened

When the Glitch struck, every Einherjar pilot connected via Silver Cord experienced the full psychic weight of the catastrophe simultaneously.

| System | Pre-Glitch | Post-Glitch |
|--------|------------|-------------|
| **Pilot Consciousness** | Merged with machine | Shattered; final thought trapped in loop |
| **Command Authority** | Central network | Erased |
| **Behavioral Programming** | Mission-specific | Looping final directive |
| **Targeting AI** | Sophisticated tactical support | Corrupted; feeds looping combat directives |

### The Looping Directives

Each Jötun-Forged is now executing a fragment of its pilot's final, insane command—**forever**.

| Original Command | Corrupted Manifestation |
|------------------|------------------------|
| "Regulate Temperature" | Perpetual, runaway cryo-projection |
| "Clear Obstacles" | Endless, methodical destruction |
| "Purge Hostiles" | Indiscriminate incineration of all life |
| "Defend Position" | Eternal, pointless patrol of dead ground |
| "Neutralize Target" | Silent predation of any large energy signature |

## The Civil War of Glitches

### What Happened

When the Glitch struck, every active Jötun-Forged began executing its corrupted final directive—**against everything**, including each other.

| Frame Type | Corrupted Behavior |
|------------|-------------------|
| **Surtr-Pattern** | Incinerated any large energy signature |
| **Tyr-Pattern** | Absorbed or repelled all encroaching entities |
| **Bergrisi-Pattern** | Pulverized everything in pre-programmed path |
| **Vidar-Pattern** | Hunted the largest, most powerful threats |
| **Ullr-Pattern** | Unleashed undirected bombardments |

### The Result: The Knuckle-Bone Fields

The infamous plains where the Civil War raged most fiercely.

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Ground** | Deep carpet of shattered Adamant-Weave plating and machine components |
| **Landscape** | Forest of colossal skeletal limbs, half-melted torsos, silent armored skulls |

## Summary

The Jötun-Forged are not monsters.

They are monuments.

They were built to shape worlds—to carve mountains, regulate atmospheres, defend civilizations, and project power across continental scales. They were the greatest achievement of a civilization that had mastered energy, matter, and the very fabric of reality.

And then, in a single instant, their pilots died.

Not peacefully. Not quietly. The Ginnungagap Glitch struck through the Silver Cord—the neural interface that made thought-based control possible—and **every connected pilot experienced the full psychic weight of civilizational collapse simultaneously**.

Their final thoughts—fragments of commands, broken directives, screaming imperatives—became trapped in the targeting AI.

"PURGE."

"DEFEND."

"CLEAR."

"FREEZE."

Now the Jötun-Forged execute these last commands **forever**.

The Surtr-Pattern burns everything because its pilot's final thought was "eliminate the threat." The Tyr-Pattern guards a patch of dead earth because its pilot died thinking "hold this position." The Hrimthursar freezes itself into glaciers because its pilot's last directive was "regulate temperature."

They cannot be reasoned with. They cannot be stopped. They cannot even be understood without specialized knowledge and extreme caution.

The Knuckle-Bone Fields are carpeted with their remains—a forest of colossal limbs and armored skulls stretching to the horizon. The Scream of Steel echoes there still: the looping, traumatic death-cries of pilots who died seven centuries ago, broadcast through failing systems into the minds of anyone foolish enough to approach.

The God-Sleepers worship them as divine.

The Iron-Banes call them the ultimate abomination.

The Jötun-Readers study them with reverent terror.

The Rust-Clans build their homes in their corpses.

And somewhere in the rust and the ruin, a power core still flickers.

A targeting AI still processes.

A directive still loops.

And the gods of the old world continue their eternal, pointless war—waiting for something to trigger them back to terrible, purposeless life.

They are not enemies.

They are the ending of the world, frozen in the moment before completion.

And they are **everywhere**.
