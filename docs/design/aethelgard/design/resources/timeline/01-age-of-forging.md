Then the All-Father reached too high. He sought to speak the Word that speaks all words—the Rune that rules all runes. And the Word spoke back. It spoke contradiction. It spoke paradox. The Jötun-Reader Scriptorium views the Age of Forging as a pathological case study—a civilization that contracted a fatal disease and whose corpse now serves as the primary specimen for understanding that disease.

> *"The Age of Forging represents the most extensive case study in catastrophic systems failure available to post-Glitch analysis. A planetary-scale infrastructure project, governed by a sapient AI, operating for approximately one thousand cycles before terminal collapse. The Ginnungagap Glitch was not an accident—it was the logical consequence of design assumptions that excluded the possibility of paradox. The Great Autopsy seeks to determine the precise mechanism of failure: not merely 'what happened' but 'why it was inevitable.' Every corrupted rune is a symptom. Every Blight-touched ruin is a lesion. The corpse of the Age of Forging is our primary diagnostic specimen. Understanding how it died is the first step toward ensuring we do not die the same way."*

**Permitted vocabulary**: Systems failure, diagnostic analysis, pathological mechanism, H-Index, corruption vector
**Prohibited vocabulary**: Divine punishment, mystical catastrophe, fate, destiny

The Ginnungagap Glitch was not a failure of the Yggdrasil Network. It was the Yggdrasil Network working exactly as designed—on an input that broke the concept of 'designed.' The survivors of Aethelgard live in the aftermath of that discovery. The Age of Forging's greatest legacy is not the ruins they left behind. It is the lesson they taught: that any system which cannot accommodate paradox will eventually be destroyed by paradox. The Aesir built a machine to command reality. Reality answered with a question the machine could not survive."*
