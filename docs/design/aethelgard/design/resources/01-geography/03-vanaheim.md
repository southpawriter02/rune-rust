## Summary

Vanaheim answers the question: **What happens when the Garden fails?**

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Realm** | Vanaheim |
| **Purpose** | Life Creation (Genesis Engine; planetary-scale bio-engineering) |
| **Post-Glitch** | The Verdant Hell |
| **Biome Theme** | Vertical stratification; continuous bio-creation crisis; beautiful toxicity |
| **Core Hazard** | Un-Womb (ceaseless creation without constraint) |
| **Strategic Value** | Living laboratory for Blight bio-corruption; Project Idunn archives |

Vanaheim is not a ruin that has surrendered to entropy.

It is a **garden that has gone to war** against the concept of gardens having limits.

The Genesis Engine did not stop creating life—it stopped caring what kind of life it created. The Un-Womb produces 200-400 kg of novel Blight-Spawn every day. The Canopy Sea survives because the Grove-Clans learned to ride the bloom cycles. The Under-growth belongs to the Unraveled—the Lost Kin whose bodies are still being rewritten by the garden that consumed them.

Paradise became pathology. The perfect system did not die—it became something that remembers perfection and cannot stop trying to recreate it.

> *"We do not hate the garden. We do not fear it. We tend it, as best we can, knowing that it will never be tame again. The Vanir tried to control life. Life disagreed. We are the children of that disagreement, and we have learned to live with the argument."*
> — Grove-Clan Druidic Council teaching, Heartwood Yggr-7

> *"The Under-growth is not a place. It is a process. It is what happens when creation has no editor. We do not go there to conquer it. We go there to remember what we almost became."*
> — Ljósálfar warden blessing, Rope Court Market Crown, Year 783 PG
