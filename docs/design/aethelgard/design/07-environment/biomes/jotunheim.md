# Jötunheim — The Industrial Graveyard

> *"Do not mistake the silence for emptiness. Jötunheim is not a wasteland—it is a factory floor the size of a continent, flash-frozen at the exact moment of catastrophic, system-wide failure. The mountains are slag heaps. The forests are tangled steel. The only sound is the wind singing a mournful dirge through a dead god's ribcage."*
> — Rust-Clan Saga, "The Iron Harvest"

---

## 1. Overview

**Jötunheim** is the industrial decay biome—the graveyard of the Old World's greatest ambitions. These are the vast, silent factory floors, the colossal assembly yards, and the crumbling transport networks where the god-like Jötun-Forged were built, maintained, and ultimately, where they fell. The entire biome is built in, around, and through the **titanic corpses of dead metal giants**.

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Designation** | Deck 06 — Primary Heavy Manufacturing & Assembly |
| **Verticality** | Roots/Trunk (Z=-2 to Z=+2, Surface/Sub-Surface Industrial) |
| **Population** | Large (10,000+ in Utgard; thousands more transient) |
| **Strategic Importance** | Critical — Largest accessible Adamant-Weave reserve |
| **Hazard Classification** | Environmental/Mechanical/Respiratory |

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Technology, Not Mythology (Domain 3/4 Compliance).** Jötun-Forged are Pre-Glitch mega-scale construction equipment, not mythical titans. God-Sleeper Cultists are cargo cultists worshipping technology they do not understand. The "Scream of Steel" is a looping cognitive broadcast from dead pilots, not supernatural haunting.

## 2. Pre-Glitch Function (Domain 4 Compliant)

Before the Ginnungagap Glitch, Jötunheim was the **primary heavy manufacturing center** for the Aesir Technocracy—a continent-spanning factory complex dedicated to the construction and maintenance of the colossal Jötun-Forged.

### 2.1 The Great Looms

The heart of Jötunheim's industrial capacity:

| Component | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| **Urðr-Class Grand Assembly Complexes** | Nine surviving mega-factories rising 200–400 meters; primary Jötun-Forged construction sites |
| **Coolant Channels** | Continent-spanning liquid metal cooling systems; now the "Dry Rivers" |
| **Conveyor Networks** | Automated material transport across hundreds of kilometers |
| **Marshaling Yards** | Staging areas for completed units; now the Ash-Blown Wastes |

### 2.2 The Jötun-Forged Program

| Unit Type | Function | Scale |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| **Bergrisi (Mountain-Giants)** | Heavy construction; terraforming | 80–120 meters |
| **Einherjar-Class Frames** | Combat/security platforms | 15–30 meters |
| **Jötun-Haulers** | Heavy cargo transport | 40–60 meters |
| **Volund-Pattern Repair-Constructs** | Maintenance automation | 3–8 meters |

### 2.3 Pre-Glitch Workforce

The human element of Jötunheim consisted of low-status maintenance personnel:

| Role | Function |
|------|----------|
| **Technicians** | Managing human-accessible systems |
| **Foremen** | Logistics and crew coordination |
| **Housing Staff** | Family housing integrated into factory infrastructure |
| **Maintenance Crews** | Practical expertise in industrial machinery |

> [!NOTE]
> These workers possessed practical expertise but no access to high-level schematics or runic programming. They would become the ancestors of the Rust-Clans.

### 2.4 Architectural Philosophy

> *"Jötunheim's program was to convert raw material into gods. Every structure was a temple to scale, every process a ritual of creation."*

| Element | Design Purpose |
|---------|----------------|
| **Assembly Floors** | Open-plan construction bays sized for 100-meter units |
| **Gantry Networks** | Multi-level access for human technicians |
| **Coolant Infrastructure** | Liquid metal channels managing thermal loads |
| **Power Grid** | Continent-spanning Aetheric distribution network |

## 3. The Cataclysm: The Civil War of Glitches

When the Ginnungagap Glitch struck, Jötunheim experienced a unique catastrophe—the **"Civil War of Glitches"**—as the Jötun-Forged turned on each other in a system-wide logic failure.

| Phase | Event | Effect |
|-------|-------|--------|
| **Initial Cascade** | IFF (Identify Friend/Foe) protocols corrupt | Jötun-Forged begin attacking each other |
| **The Knuckle-Bone War** | Full-scale mechanized conflict | Continent-spanning battle between corrupted units |
| **Flash-Freeze** | Power grid collapse | All activity ceases mid-motion |
| **The Silence** | Systems fail permanently | Jötunheim becomes a frozen battlefield |

### 3.1 The Knuckle-Bone War

> *"For seventeen minutes, the factory floor became a battlefield. Bergrisi grappled with Bergrisi. Einherjar frames tore each other apart. The Scream of Steel began—and has never stopped."*

The corrupted IFF protocols caused every Jötun-Forged to identify every other Jötun-Forged as hostile. The resulting seventeen-minute war ended only when the power grid collapsed, freezing the combatants mid-battle.

### 3.2 Human Survival

The Rust-Clan ancestors survived not through prepared shelters but through **improvised refuge**:

| Location | Survival Method |
|----------|-----------------|
| Maintenance tunnels | Already below the conflict |
| Assembly pit depths | Below line of fire |
| Half-finished chassis cavities | Sheltered inside the machines |
| Coolant channel bunkers | Emergency flood shelters |

> *"They were already inside the machine when it broke. That's the only reason any of them lived."*

## 4. Core Mechanics

### 4.1 No Ambient Condition

Unlike other biomes with persistent environmental effects (Golden Plague, Glimmer, etc.), Jötunheim has **physical/technological threats only**:

| Property | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Nature** | Industrial hazards replace ambient magical conditions |
| **Focus** | Tactical combat positioning; environmental awareness |
| **Effect** | No constant HP/Stress drain; punctuated danger instead |

### 4.2 Undying Dominance

High concentration of armored mechanical enemies:

| Requirement | Effect |
|-------------|--------|
| **Armor-Shredding** | Required for Draugr Juggernauts and heavy frames |
| **Physical Soak** | All Undying have high armor values |
| **Energy Vulnerability** | Electrical attacks bypass mechanical resistance |

### 4.3 Extreme Verticality

Walking on/through fallen Jötun-Forged creates multi-level battlefields:

| Layer | Distribution | Content |
|-------|--------------|---------|
| **Trunk (Z=0 to Z=+2)** | 70% | Factory floors, gantry walks, corpse exteriors |
| **Roots (Z=-1 to Z=-2)** | 30% | Maintenance tunnels, coolant channels, sub-assembly |

## 6. The Five Zones

Jötunheim comprises five distinct terrain regions:

### 6.1 The Knuckle-Bone Fields

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Terrain** | Deep carpet of shattered Adamant-Weave plating and machine components |
| **Features** | Forest of colossal skeletal limbs, half-melted torsos, silent armored skulls |
| **Hazard** | "Scream of Steel"—looping traumatic echoes of dead pilots (Type Alpha CPS) |
| **Wildlife** | Prime hunting ground for Svin-fylking; nesting territory of Hraesvelgr's Kin |
| **Resources** | Highest concentration of salvageable Adamant-Weave |

> *"The fields stretch to the horizon—a forest of metal bones, each one the remains of something that was built to last forever."*

### 6.2 The Ash-Blown Wastes

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Terrain** | Vast, flat expanses of hard-packed industrial waste and metallic dust |
| **Former Use** | Primary transit and marshaling yards |
| **Current Use** | Highways for nomadic Rust-Clan Rigs |
| **Hazard** | No cover from corrosive Rust-Devil storms or roving predators |
| **Navigation** | Fastest travel routes; highest exposure risk |

### 6.3 The Rust-Flows (The Dry Rivers)

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Terrain** | Ancient, continent-spanning coolant channels and conveyor-belt trenches |
| **Condition** | Coolant boiled away; floors now thick sludge of toxic residue |
| **Navigation** | Often the safest and most direct routes of travel |
| **Hazard** | Territory of Conveyor-Serpents; infested with Rust-Rot Moss colonies |
| **Cover** | Trench walls provide shelter from Rust-Devils |

### 6.4 The Ginnungagap Coolant Chasm

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Terrain** | Festering river of iridescent purple-green chemical sludge |
| **Corruption** | Berkano (ᛒ) corruption |
| **Penetration Rate** | Dissolves Grade-B steel at 2mm/hour |
| **Hazard** | Toxic vapors cause Type Beta CPS onset within 10 minutes |
| **Navigation** | Impassable; requires bridging or lengthy detours |

### 6.5 The Unfinished Gods

The most haunting feature of Jötunheim:

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Nature** | Incomplete Jötun-Forged frozen mid-assembly |
| **Condition** | Torsos fused to cradles; arms reaching for components that will never arrive |
| **Atmosphere** | Silent, unnerving reminder of the moment the world broke |
| **Resources** | Pristine components; never activated (no Forlorn contamination) |

> *"A torso lies fused to its assembly cradle. A completed arm reaches for a component that will never arrive. These silent, incomplete giants are a constant, unnerving reminder of the moment the world broke."*

|------|----------|------|

| **Grip-Rune** (Nauthiz ᚾ) | Enhanced grip | May make it impossible to let go |

| Faction | Approach |

| **Jötun-Readers** | Won't use rune unless they understand mechanism |
| **Iron-Banes** | Won't use rune unless theologically pure |

## Summary

Jötunheim is not a wasteland. It is a **crime scene the size of a continent**—a factory floor frozen at the exact moment of system-wide failure, preserved in rust and silence for centuries. The giants lie where they fell, mid-swing, mid-stride, mid-assembly. The coolant has boiled away. The conveyors have stopped. The only movement is the wind, carrying its cargo of corrosive dust and the distant, echoing Scream of Steel.

And yet.

The Rust-Clans have made this graveyard into a home. Their Rigs crawl across the Ash-Blown Wastes, following seasonal salvage routes their ancestors mapped in the first years after the Glitch. Utgard rises inside a dead god's ribcage, a vertical slum of ten thousand souls trading scrap and stories in the shadow of the unfinished Aurgelmir. The Dvergr send their trade delegations year after year, purchasing the bones of the old world to forge the tools of the new.

Jötunheim is the largest accessible reserve of Adamant-Weave in Aethelgard. Fifty to eighty years of salvage, if the extraction rates hold. The economic foundation of the post-Glitch recovery.

The Rust-Clans call it home.

The Dvergr call it essential.

The Iron-Banes call it tainted.

The God-Sleepers kneel before the frozen giants, waiting for an awakening that will never come.

And the Scream of Steel echoes across the Knuckle-Bone Fields, a looping broadcast of agony from pilots who died seven hundred years ago, still trapped in the moment their machines turned on each other and the world ended.

Jötunheim does not forget. It cannot. The silence is not peace—it is a pause button that was never released, a moment of catastrophic failure preserved in rust and metal and the endless, howling wind.

The factory is still waiting. It just doesn't know what for.
