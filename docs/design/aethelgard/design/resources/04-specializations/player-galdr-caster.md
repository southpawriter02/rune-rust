# The Galdr-Caster — The Command-Line User

> *"A Galdr-Caster does not ask the Aether for permission. They do not negotiate with the static. They plant their feet, draw breath, and speak the word of the sun so loudly that reality has no choice but to obey—even if just for a moment, even if the world screams back."*
> — Archivist Theron, Scriptorium-Primus

---

## Overview

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Archetype** | Mystic |
| **Role** | Arcane Artillery, Elemental Devastator, Glass Cannon |
| **Path Type** | Coherent (with High Trauma Risk) |
| **Primary Attribute** | WILL |
| **Secondary Attribute** | WITS |
| **Resource System** | Aether Pool |
| **Trauma Economy Risk** | High |
| **Unlock Cost** | 10 PP (higher cost due to power and risk) |

The **Galdr-Caster** is not a subtle programmer or a heretical conduit.

They are a **command-line power user**.

They have learned to directly execute the raw, primal commands of the Futhark—the source code of reality—through powerful, resonant vocalizations known as *galdrar*. Their magic is an act of overwhelming, inefficient, and spectacular force. Where the Rúnasmiðr carefully inscribes firewalled scripts and the Echo-Caller weaponizes the static's scream, the Galdr-Caster simply **shouts louder than the noise**.

They are the arcane equivalent of a brute-force hacker—screaming system commands at a broken operating system and hoping it obeys before something catastrophic happens.

To choose the Galdr-Caster is to embrace the fantasy of the **arcane artillery piece**—the elemental devastator whose saga is written in scorched earth, shattered ice, and ozone-scented air.

Your power is the highest in the game. Your survivability is among the lowest.

## Core Philosophy: Brute-Forcing Broken Code

### The Art of Galdr

| Principle | Expression |
|-----------|------------|
| **Command, Not Interpretation** | Galdr is forcing the Aether to obey, not listening to it |
| **Volume Over Subtlety** | Shout the command so loudly that the static cannot drown it out |
| **Power Over Safety** | Maximum damage potential at maximum personal risk |
| **The Primal Commands** | Fire, Ice, Lightning—the elemental executables of the Futhark |

The Galdr-Caster's power comes from a philosophy of overwhelming force: where others negotiate with the corrupted Aether or carefully firewall their inscriptions, the Galdr-Caster simply **commands**.

They do not seek to understand, purify, or bargain with the tainted Aether. They overwhelm the Blight's psychic static with sheer force of will, forcing the chaotic energy to manifest as devastating gouts of fire, shards of ice, and bolts of lightning.

> *"The system can be forced. The Galdr-caster is not a subtle programmer; they are a power user who has learned how to directly execute the raw, primal commands of the Futhark through vocalization."*

### The Coherent Mystic with High Risk

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Path Type** | Coherent (commanding the Aether, not weaponizing entropy) |
| **Risk Level** | High (Corruption accumulation, interruption backlash, Wild Magic Surges) |
| **Corruption Source** | High (every cast is an unshielded interface with tainted Aether) |
| **Stress Source** | High (interrupted chants cause massive psychic backlash) |

The Galdr-Caster is technically a **Coherent** specialization—their art is dangerous but not inherently heretical like the Rust-Witch or Blót-Priest. They do not deliberately channel entropy or make blood sacrifices.

But they have **extreme Trauma Economy exposure**. Their brute-force method is the most direct and unfiltered way to interface with the tainted Aether. Every cast is a gamble—a deliberate act of inviting the world's sickness into oneself and unleashing it upon enemies.

The corruption gain is not mystical punishment; it is *system bleed*. Each cast is an unshielded interface with tainted Aether, and the Blight's logic-corruption accumulates in the practitioner's biological systems like radiation exposure.

## Gameplay Fantasy

### Player Experience

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Core Fantasy** | Arcane artillery; the one who calls down the cataclysm |
| **Gameplay Feel** | High-risk, high-reward damage dealer with tense chant timing |
| **Success Metric** | Devastating AoE damage; exploiting elemental vulnerabilities |
| **Decision Space** | "Can my party protect me long enough to finish this incantation?" |

Playing a Galdr-Caster is about **positioning, preparation, and unleashing devastation**.

Your gameplay loop:
- **Position**: Stay in the back row, behind the wall
- **Chant**: Spend turns in vulnerable preparation, building toward your spell
- **Survive**: Depend on your party to protect you from interruption
- **Unleash**: Release battlefield-altering elemental devastation

You are the ultimate glass cannon. Your damage potential is the highest in the game—but you are extremely fragile, can only wear Light Armor, and are completely defenseless while chanting.

Every battle is a tense question: *"Can we hold the line long enough for the caster to finish?"*

> *"Playing a Galdr-caster is a game of timing and risk management, a constant question of 'Can my party protect me long enough to finish this incantation?'"*

## Core Mechanics

### Elemental Command

| Element | Rune | Name | Effect |
|---------|------|------|--------|
| **Fire** | ᛋ Sowilo | Heat-Light | Raw energy release; single-target and AoE damage |
| **Ice** | ᛁ Isa | Cold-Stillness | Imposing stasis; AoE damage with crowd control |
| **Lightning** | ᚫ Ansuz | Sky-Fire | Storm's fury; massive single-target damage with Stun |

The **Elemental Command** mechanic defines the Galdr-Caster as the premier specialist in Fire, Ice, and Lightning damage. Their toolkit is a focused arsenal of single-target and AoE spells across these three elemental families.

| Spell | Element | Damage Type | Target |
|-------|---------|-------------|--------|
| **Sowilo's Spear** | Fire | Single-target | One enemy |
| **Isa's Breath** | Ice | Cone AoE | Front Row |
| **Hagalaz's Storm** | Ice | Full AoE | All enemies |
| **Ansuz's Fury** | Lightning | Single-target + Stun | One enemy |

This makes them the ultimate tool for exploiting enemy elemental vulnerabilities—and the **Elemental Focus** passive creates vulnerability chains (Ice → Physical; Fire → Lightning).

### The Galdr Chant

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Function** | Most powerful abilities require 1-3 turns of vulnerable preparation |
| **State** | Stationary; cannot move or take other actions while chanting |
| **Interruption** | Taking damage requires WILL-based check to maintain the chant |
| **Failure** | Broken chant loses the spell, resources spent, and inflicts Psychic Stress |

The **Galdr Chant** mechanic is the Galdr-Caster's defining feature and greatest vulnerability.

> *"A chant is the Galdr-Caster forcing their will upon the corrupted Aether, a loud, unsubtle process that generates a massive disturbance in the Great Silence."*

During a chant, you are:
- Stationary (cannot move)
- Vulnerable (cannot defend or take other actions)
- A beacon (attracting the ire of Blighted foes)
- At risk (damage triggers interruption checks)

A broken chant is catastrophic: you lose the spell, the Aether spent, and suffer massive Psychic Stress as the corrupted energy backlashes through your mind.

**Chant-Discipline Protocol** (Layer 2):
- **Breath Protocol**: Counted breaths, anchor posts, metronome taps
- **Circle Geometry**: Ground marks and anchor placements to reduce interference
- **Interruption Hygiene**: Crew behaviors that protect the chant window

> *"Call the window. Keep the count. Drop volume before you drop timing."*

### Corruption Overload

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Function** | Spend accumulated Corruption as fuel for enhanced damage |
| **Trigger** | Active ability (Corruption Overload) |
| **Effect** | Next spell becomes [Overcharged]: massive bonus damage + random Glitch status |
| **Risk** | All-or-nothing gambit; depletes Corruption but commits to the attack |

The **Corruption Overload** mechanic represents the Galdr-Caster's ultimate gambit—deliberately pushing their own Blighted system to the breaking point.

> *"The Galdr-caster opens the floodgates, allowing the raw, anti-logical code they've accumulated to surge through them and into their next spell."*

This creates a unique gameplay pattern: instead of trying to minimize Corruption, the Galdr-Caster can *weaponize* it, spending their accumulated system damage for one spectacular burst of power.

## Abilities: The Elemental Lexicon

### Tier 1 — Foundational Evocation

| Ability | Type | Cost | Effect |
|---------|------|------|--------|
| **Aetheric Flow I** | Passive | — | +10% Maximum Aether Pool; +1d10 to WILL checks vs. chant interruption |
| **Sowilo's Spear** | Active | 35 Aether | Single-target Fire damage; most reliable non-chanted spell |
| **Isa's Breath** | Active (Chant 1) | 45 Aether | After 1-turn chant: moderate Ice damage to all Front Row enemies |

**Aetheric Flow I** represents the Galdr-Caster's body becoming a natural conduit for the Aether—a deeper well of corrupted power from which to draw. The bonus to interruption resistance is critical for protecting your chants.

**Sowilo's Spear** is the bread-and-butter damage spell. You speak a single, sharp word of power, hurling a shimmering spear of pure solar energy. No chant required—this is your reliable fallback when you can't afford vulnerability.

> *"The Galdr-caster speaks a single, sharp word of power, forcing the tainted Aether into the form of a shimmering spear of raw energy."*

**Isa's Breath** is the first chanted spell. You begin a low, humming chant, the air frosting over as you wrestle the chaotic Aether into a state of cold, ordered stasis. After one turn, you exhale a cone of biting, glacial frost.

### Tier 2 — Advanced Elementalism

| Ability | Type | Cost | Effect |
|---------|------|------|--------|
| **Galdr of Breaking** | Active (Chant 1) | 50 Aether | After 1-turn chant: low Physical damage + **[Shattered]** debuff (reduces Soak for 3 rounds) |
| **Hagalaz's Storm** | Active (Chant 2) | 65 Aether | After 2-turn chant: high Ice damage to ALL enemies (both rows) |
| **Elemental Focus** | Passive | — | Ice spells apply Physical Vulnerability (1 round); Fire spells apply Lightning Vulnerability (1 round) |

**Galdr of Breaking** is the armor-shredder. You chant a discordant, resonant galdr designed to find and exploit microscopic fractures in a target's armor. The **[Shattered]** debuff significantly reduces Soak, setting up your party's physical damage dealers.

> *"The sound wave is a focused burst of entropy, a command that accelerates the target's decay."*

**Hagalaz's Storm** is your first true AoE devastation. A longer, more complex chant unleashes a localized magical hailstorm—high Ice damage to every enemy on the battlefield. Two turns of vulnerability for battlefield-clearing power.

**Elemental Focus** creates vulnerability chains. Blast enemies with Ice, then your Warrior deals bonus Physical damage. Blast with Fire, then follow up with Lightning for even more devastation. This passive transforms the Galdr-Caster into a force multiplier for the entire party.

### Tier 3 — Mastery of Evocation

| Ability | Type | Cost | Effect |
|---------|------|------|--------|
| **Ansuz's Fury** | Active (Chant 2) | 75 Aether | After 2-turn chant: massive Lightning damage to single target + high **[Stunned]** chance |
| **Unstable Power** | Passive | — | Increased Wild Magic Surge chance; surges more likely to be beneficial **[Overcharged]** effects |
| **Corruption Overload** | Active | 10 Aether | Spend Corruption as resource; next spell becomes Overcharged with massive bonus damage + random Glitch status |
| **Will of the Conduit** | Passive | — | Significant bonus to WILL checks vs. interruption; +1d10 to resist Psychic Stress from own spells |

**Ansuz's Fury** is the single-target nuke. You call down the fury of the storm itself, summoning a massive bolt of lightning from the heavens. Massive damage plus a high chance to leave the target **[Stunned]**—unable to act on their next turn.

> *"The Galdr-caster calls down the fury of the storm itself, summoning a massive bolt of lightning from the heavens."*

**Unstable Power** embraces the chaos. The chance for Wild Magic Surges increases—but when surges occur, they're more likely to be beneficial **[Overcharged]** effects rather than catastrophic backfires. Riding the edge of the system crash, turning potential errors into overwhelming force.

**Corruption Overload** is the all-or-nothing gambit. You deliberately push your own Blighted system to the breaking point, spending a large amount of accumulated Corruption to supercharge your next spell. The result is **[Overcharged]**: massive bonus damage, potentially of a random element, with a high chance to apply Glitch-based status effects.

**Will of the Conduit** is the defensive capstone passive. Your will becomes a fortress against the constant psychic assault of your own magic. Significant bonus to interruption resistance and Psychic Stress mitigation from your own spells.

### Capstone — Ragnarök's Herald

| Component | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **Cost** | 100 Aether |
| **Chant** | 3 Turns |
| **Frequency** | Once per combat |
| **Effect** | Storm of meteors, lightning, and ice shards—massive mixed-elemental damage to ALL enemies |
| **Backlash** | Massive, irresistible Corruption + Psychic Stress; caster is **[Stunned]** for 1 turn |

The capstone **Ragnarök's Herald** represents the Galdr-Caster becoming a living conduit for the world-ending cataclysm—their voice a symphony of fire, ice, and lightning, a promise of total, glorious system failure.

> *"The Galdr-Caster becomes a living conduit for the world-ending cataclysm, their voice a symphony of fire, ice, and lightning—a promise of total, glorious system failure."*

Three turns of chanting. Three turns of absolute vulnerability. And then—devastation.

The aftermath is brutal: massive, irresistible Corruption and Psychic Stress as your own system struggles to reboot from the overwhelming power surge. You are left **[Stunned]** for your next turn, completely defenseless.

This is the ultimate glass cannon moment. Everything depends on whether your party can protect you for three full turns—and whether the devastation is worth the cost.

## Systemic Integration

### Trauma Economy Interaction

| Source | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| **Baseline Spells** | Moderate Corruption per cast (unshielded Aether interface) |
| **Chanted Spells** | Increased Corruption (deeper interface with the Blight) |
| **Interrupted Chants** | Massive Psychic Stress (psychic backlash) |
| **Ragnarök's Herald** | Catastrophic Corruption + Stress (system overload) |
| **Wild Magic Surges** | Variable negative effects (Unstable Power risk) |

The Galdr-Caster accumulates Corruption **faster than any other Coherent specialization**. Their brute-force method creates constant system bleed—the Blight's logic-corruption accumulating with every cast.

### Mitigation Tools

| Tool | Effect |
|------|--------|
| **Aetheric Flow I** | +1d10 to interruption resistance (protects chants) |
| **Will of the Conduit** | Significant interruption bonus + Stress resistance |
| **Corruption Overload** | Converts accumulated Corruption into damage (controlled release) |
| **Party Protection** | Tank + Controller keeping threats away from the caster |

The Galdr-Caster has some built-in mitigation through WILL-based passives, but their primary defense is **party coordination**. They are entirely dependent on their allies to survive.

### Party Synergies

| Ally | Synergy |
|------|---------|
| **Skjaldmær** | Tank holds the wall; Taunt keeps enemies away from the caster |
| **Hlekkr-master** | Controller Roots or Stuns threats that break through |
| **Atgeir-Wielder** | Reach weapon holds lane pressure; protects the chant window |
| **Bone-Setter** | Manages aftermath shock; CPS checks post-chant; Coherence medicine |

> *"Wall Pairing: Skjaldmær sets board at windward hip; Atgeir-Wielder holds lane pressure so the window survives."*

The Galdr-Caster is the **party's artillery**—entirely dependent on their allies. A Galdr-Caster without a Tank is a dead Galdr-Caster.

### Counter-Synergies

| Situation | Problem |
|-----------|---------|
| **No Tank** | Without protection, chants will be interrupted constantly |
| **High-mobility enemies** | Enemies that bypass the front line threaten the caster directly |
| **Silence effects** | [Silenced] status completely shuts down Galdr (verbal components required) |
| **Element-resistant enemies** | Narrow toolkit (Fire/Ice/Lightning only) struggles against resistant foes |

## Lore Integration

### Chant-Discipline Tradition

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Historical Roots** | Temple hymns adapted into field procedures after the Glitch |
| **Codification** | Reader monasteries formalized breath protocols and circle geometry |
| **Modern Practice** | Disciplined practitioners interfacing with fractured Aether through voice |
| **Social Function** | Artillery under oath; problem-key for environmental locks; civic ceremony |

The Galdr-Caster tradition evolved from pre-Glitch temple hymns. Early post-Glitch singers adapted these into field procedures: counted breaths, anchor posts, and metronome taps. Reader monasteries codified these into reproducible chant protocols.

Surviving ledgers align three stable families of calls—heat-light (Sowilo), cold-stillness (Isa), and sky-fire (Ansuz)—each with characteristic tells and aftermaths documented in clinic notes.

## Summary

The Galdr-Caster is the **arcane artillery piece** of Aethelgard.

Where others negotiate with the corrupted Aether or carefully firewall their inscriptions, the Galdr-Caster simply commands. They plant their feet, draw breath, and speak the words of the sun so loudly that reality has no choice but to obey.

Their power is the highest in the game. Ragnarök's Herald—three turns of chanting, then fire and ice and lightning devastating everything on the battlefield—is the ultimate expression of magical force.

But they are the ultimate glass cannon. Low HP. Light Armor only. Completely defenseless while chanting. A broken chant is catastrophic: lost spell, lost resources, massive Psychic Stress backlash.

The Chant mechanic creates the Galdr-Caster's defining tension: every powerful spell requires turns of vulnerable preparation. Every battle becomes a question of *"can we protect the caster long enough?"*

Their Corruption Overload mechanic creates a unique risk/reward dynamic: instead of minimizing Corruption, they can weaponize it—spending accumulated system damage for one spectacular burst of power.

They are entirely dependent on their party. A Galdr-Caster without a Tank is a dead Galdr-Caster. A Galdr-Caster with a coordinated team is a force of nature.

*They plant their feet.*
*They draw breath.*
*They speak the word of fire.*
*And for one terrible moment, the world listens.*
