# The Knuckle-Bone Fields — The Great Graveyard of Jötunheim

> *"The old ones who lived inside the machines tell us that when the gods went mad, the iron giants turned upon each other. For days beyond counting they fought—grinding, tearing, crushing—until nothing remained but the bones of the fallen, heaped so deep that a man could walk for a hundred days and never touch true ground. They say the giants left behind a bounty of iron flesh for their children to harvest. They speak of it with a grim and holy reverence. This is not a metaphor."*
> — Rust-Clan oral tradition, "The Lay of the Iron Graveyard"

The Knuckle-Bone Fields are a colossal, infamous geographical feature occupying approximately **400,000 square kilometers** of Jötunheim's central plains—an area equivalent to pre-Glitch Germany. This is the vast ossuary where the **Civil War of Glitches** raged most fiercely, where thousands of Jötun-Forged mechanoids fought each other to mutual annihilation in the hours following the Ginnungagap Glitch.

This is not a battlefield. It is the **continent-scale corpse of industrial civilization**—and its bones are worth fortunes.

| Phase | Event |

## Summary

The Knuckle-Bone Fields answer the question: **What remains when gods of iron kill each other?**

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Geographic Type** | Industrial Ossuary |
| **Area** | ~400,000 km² |
| **Terrain** | 8–15 meters compacted industrial wreckage |
| **Origin** | Civil War of Glitches (72-hour mutual annihilation) |
| **Strategic Value** | Single largest Adamant-Weave reserve; 50–80 years at current extraction |
| **Primary Hazards** | Iron-Slides, Rust-Devils, Rusting Lung, Scream of Steel (Type Alpha CPS) |

The Fields are not a wasteland.

They are the **largest salvage yard in the world**—the continent-scale corpse of industrial civilization, where thousands of Jötun-Forged mechanoids ground each other into the dust that now fills a scavenger's lungs. The Rust-Clans call it home. The Iron-Banes call it cursed ground. The Dvergr call it a business opportunity. The Jötun-Readers call it a pathological goldmine.

To walk the Knuckle-Bone Fields is to walk on the bones of dead giants—8 to 15 meters deep, razor-sharp, unstable, and haunted by the psychic echoes of ten thousand pilots who died screaming in machines that no longer knew friend from foe. The horizon is perfectly flat in all directions, rust-red ground fading into rust-orange sky, and somewhere beneath your feet is enough Adamant-Weave to fuel civilization for another century.

If you can live long enough to dig it out.
