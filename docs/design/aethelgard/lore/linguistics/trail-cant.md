### 2.1 The Payload Triad

## Â§3. Security Architecture: The Shout and the Whisper

Trail-Cant's survival value made it a target. Bandits learned the primary markings and began creating false warningsâ€”lures to trap travelers or diversions to protect their territories. The Guild responded with layered authentication. [FRAG7, FRAG12, FRAG15]

### 3.1 The Lie-Mark Problem

A fundamental Guild lesson: "A single, obvious, and perfectly carved Mark is almost always a lie." A too-clean symbol in an obvious location signals either a bandit trap or a scavenger's territorial bluff. True Rangers leave imperfect marks in imperfect places.

### 3.2 The Shout/Whisper Protocol

Every authentic message consists of two components:

- **The Shout:** The visible primary markâ€”loud, public, easily read
- **The Whisper:** A hidden qualifier mark, placed a few paces away, often in a different medium (chalk when the Shout is carved, scratch when the Shout is painted)

The Whisper confirms the Shout's authenticity. Without it, the Shout is suspect.

### 3.3 The Shifting Key

To prevent hostile factions from learning the Whisper's location and form, the Guild employs the Shifting Keyâ€”a secret protocol decided annually at the Great Moot by the Way-Wardens and transmitted only through trusted Guild channels. The Key determines:

- Whisper placement distance from Shout
- Whisper symbol variations for the current year
- Emergency override signals indicating compromised routes

> *"You don't say names," the veterans teach. "Names can be stolen. Marks can be forged. But the Keyâ€”the Key is a promise between Rangers, and promises don't echo."* [FRAG12]
